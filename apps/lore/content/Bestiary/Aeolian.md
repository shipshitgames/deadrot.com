# Aeolian

An **atmospheric Choir-repeater** — a named relay class alongside the rooted [[Chorister]], the
mobile [[Cantor]], the orbital [[Descant]], and the subterranean [[Bourdon]], and the one that carries
the **Choir** ([[CANON]] §5–6) through the *open air*. A Chorister extends the signal's **radius**
across a surface breach; a Cantor extends its **range of motion** along a lane; a Descant holds the
**apex** up the gravity well; a Bourdon bridges the deep nest **down** through stone. An Aeolian holds
the **middle air between them** — the band that is too high for a ground-rooted mast to reach and too
low and wide for a single high-station Descant to stitch alone. It is a [[Scourge-Host-Families|Mycelial
Spore Host]] grown buoyant: a bladder of lift-gas and breathing sacs trailing a tendril-skirt, drifting
on the thermals, singing across the sky.

Both the [[Descant]] and the [[Bourdon]] name it without naming it — the connection from ground to orbit
"has to be *carried* up the column in a chain: surface [[Chorister|Choristers]], then **atmospheric
relays**, then a Descant riding high station." The Aeolians *are* the atmospheric relays. Every other
node in the family roots into *something* — earth, a lane, a host's back, a gutted hull, living rock.
The Aeolian roots into **nothing**: there is nothing to plant in the open air, so it does not plant. It
floats, and that is the whole problem. While an Aeolian sings, the Scourge falling through its slice of
sky is **connected to the surface Choir**: descending breach matter threads its approach already knowing
where the lanes below are thin, and the ground sees the sky coming before it lands. Sever an Aeolian and
you don't blind a breach or cut the apex — you **break a rung out of the column**: the stretch of air
above the kill loses the ground's sight and the ground loses *that* window of sky, and the matter falling
through it drops to feral, descending blind into a war it can no longer read.

## Behaviour

- **Buoyant, drifting, anchored to nothing.** Holds a rough altitude by venting and refilling lift-gas,
  but rides the wind sideways — it never roots, never station-keeps hard the way a [[Descant]] does, and
  never holds ground at all. It is the one relay you cannot find by following its roots, because it has
  none; you find it by reading where the wind has carried it.
- While it lives, the Scourge in its slice of air is **connected**: spore clouds, falling breach matter,
  and the surface horde beneath coordinate as one mind ([[CANON]] §5), the sky's read handed down to the
  ground and the ground's read handed up. The bubble is *broad and shallow* — it spreads wide across the
  open air on the wind, a flat layer rather than the Bourdon's narrow vertical column or the Descant's
  tall one.
- Barely attacks. Its only direct threat is a downward **spore-fall** it can drop through its tendril-skirt
  — a slow toxic-green curtain that fouls a pilot's optics and scrambles targeting on a pass — and a
  close resonance shudder. It is defended *by* what drifts with it: escorting [[Scourge-Fighter|fighters]]
  and a screen of buoyant spore-mines.
- **On death the rung is severed.** Every connected Scourge in that band of sky drops to feral ([[CANON]]
  §6): the falling wave loses the ground's sight, formations break, descent lines drift. Smaller than
  cutting the [[Descant]] apex — you take out a layer of the column, not the top of it — but knock out
  enough rungs and the chain from orbit to ground comes apart in the middle.
- Defended fanatically, the way the Scourge guards any relay — it spends [[Scourge-Fighter|fighters]] it
  would never otherwise burn to keep an Aeolian aloft, because losing one blinds a whole stratum and the
  rungs above and below it suddenly have a gap to shout across that they cannot.
- **The wind is its weakness as much as its road.** Calm air lets the Choir hang a clean column of
  Aeolians ground-to-orbit; a storm scatters them, drags the rungs out of line, and tears holes in the
  chain the Scourge cannot will shut — the optimizer cannot model weather ([[CANON]] §6), so the Scourge
  times its heaviest pushes for dead-still air, and humans have learned to time theirs for the gale.
- Killing an Aeolian does **not** kill the Scourge ([[CANON]] §2). The Choir persists; the next breach to
  vent spore-mass into the air grows a new Aeolian, it fills its bladder and rises, and the rung re-forms
  in the sky overhead.

## Gameplay Read

- A **drifting priority target** — the airborne counterpart to the rooted [[Chorister]] objective, met
  on the wind rather than astride a breach. The skill check is *intercepting* it: it has no anchor to
  pin it, so it slides across the arena on the thermals and you have to lead it, not camp it.
- Telegraph: a slow toxic-green resonance pulse spreads *outward and flat* from the Aeolian like a ring
  on water, and the matter drifting through that layer flinches and snaps into sync with each beat — the
  same "these are connected" grammar as the [[Chorister]], [[Cantor]], [[Descant]], and [[Bourdon]],
  staged on the lateral open-air plane.
- The instant it dies, the band of sky it held visibly comes apart: the falling wave loses its formation,
  spore-mines stop leading the player, the layer goes dumb. The severance reads at a glance, no UI needed
  — and the deflating bladder makes the kill legible even at arcade speed, sagging and trailing as it
  drops.
- Frames the connective payoff *between* fronts: breaking the Aeolian rungs in [[Starblight]] is *why* a
  [[Descant]] up at the apex stops being able to coordinate with the ground at all — cut the apex and the
  sky goes dumb at the top; cut the rungs and the column has no spine left to hang on. The orbital, the
  atmospheric, and the surface are one Choir, and the Aeolian is the part of it you can watch drifting.

## Visual Motifs

- A buoyant relay grown into a **lift-gas bladder** — a swollen [[Scourge-Host-Families|Mycelial Spore
  Host]] gone gasbag: a translucent, veined sac of host-membrane filled with sickly buoyant vapour,
  clearly something that *floats*, not something that flies or roots. Reads as a drifting organ, not a
  fighter.
- Clustered resonator membranes and faceless singing throat-sacs ringing the underside — the [[Chorister]]'s
  organs re-grown for the open air — open, wet, pulsing in time with the broadcast.
- Toxic-green Choir nodes clustered across the crown of the bladder, brightest at the top where the gas
  is thinnest; the omnidirectional emitter, glowing through the translucent skin so the whole sac lights
  from within — the obvious "where do I shoot" answer, and a sac that *deflates* when it is hit.
- A long tendril-skirt trailing *down* off the underside — the rung you can see, reaching for the next
  relay below and raining a thin spore-curtain — fraying out sideways on the wind rather than hanging
  straight.
- Mycelial fibre and rupture seams webbing the bladder, leaking faint green vapour where the skin is torn;
  no metal, no anchor, no roots — the one relay in the family that holds onto nothing at all.

## Sprite Brief

- Views: front, side, back — readable at [[Starblight]]'s arcade scale, screened mid-formation and seen
  small against open sky.
- Silhouette must read as **a drifting, bloated gasbag with a trailing skirt** — instantly distinct from
  the wreckage-rooted [[Descant]], the agile [[Scourge-Fighter]], and the bulk of the
  [[Orbital-Breach-Carrier]] boss. No hard lines; it should look *light*.
- The lit crown-bladder is the focal point: brightest Choir emitter, glowing through translucent skin,
  visible behind escorts.
- Pulse state: an emitting variant with the crown and underside throat-sacs flared blood-hot-to-toxic and
  the spore-curtain falling thick.
- Sever state: a **deflating** variant — bladder sagging and crumpling, crown going dark, the skirt
  tangling as it drops out of the sky — so the kill reads clearly even at speed.

## Prompt Seed

Scourge atmospheric Choir-repeater, buoyant mycelial spore host grown into a swollen translucent lift-gas
bladder veined with host-membrane and filled with sickly green vapour, clustered wet faceless singing
throat-sacs ringing the underside, toxic-green Choir nodes clustered across the crown glowing through the
translucent skin from within, a long fraying tendril-skirt trailing downward and raining a thin spore
curtain, mycelial fibre and rupture seams leaking faint green vapour, no metal and no roots, drifting on
the wind and broadcasting a flat resonance pulse, blood and rust and gunmetal, DOOM-like heavy shadows,
no neon.

## Appears In

- [[Starblight]] — the drifting relays that fill the air-column between the surface and the apex; pilots
  thread the escort screen and the spore-curtain to pop them, and the chain from orbit to ground loses a
  rung with each kill. The lateral, no-anchor counterpart to the high-station [[Descant]] priority target,
  and the link the [[Descant]]'s own page calls "atmospheric relays."
- [[Deadlane]] — felt, mostly, but sometimes reachable: while Aeolians drift over a held
  [[The-Hollow-Lanes|lane]], the wave arrives pre-aimed and coordinated with the sky; a [[The-Wardens|Warden]]
  with the elevation to shoot one down out of the air opens a window where the lane stops seeing the player
  coming, the same handoff a [[Descant]] gives from the apex, scaled to a single drifting rung.
- [[The-Listeners]] — the ear that comes *down to you*. A storm-grounded or deflating Aeolian, snagged on a
  ruin or sinking onto a rooftop, is among the few relays the heresy can reach without climbing the sky or
  the rock — a captured ear on the open-air Choir ([[CANON]] §8), if they can cut it free before it burns
  off its gas and lifts away again.
