# Bourdon

**At a glance:** [[Scourge]] subterranean Choir-repeater · [[Scourge-Host-Families|Bone Titan Host]] rooted into descent rock · severs the deep-to-surface Choir column when destroyed · appears in [[Scourge-Survivors]] / [[Deadlane]] / [[The-Listeners]].

A **subterranean Choir-repeater** — the fourth named relay class after the rooted [[Chorister]],
the mobile [[Cantor]], and the orbital [[Descant]], and the one that carries the **Choir**
([[CANON]] §5–6) *up the rock*. A Chorister extends the signal's **radius** across a surface
breach; a Cantor extends its **range of motion** along a lane; a Descant extends it **vertically
upward**, up the gravity well to orbit. A Bourdon extends it **vertically downward** — it bridges
the deep source-nest to the surface front through solid stone. It is a [[Scourge-Host-Families|Bone
Titan Host]] grown into the throat-walls of the descent, a vast buried frame droning so low you
feel it in the rock before you hear it.

The signal is *made* at the bottom — [[CANON|the Choir Node]] in [[Perdition]], the densest
repeater-heart on the war-map — but the Choir's radius can no more cross a mile of stone than it
can cross vacuum. The connection has to be *carried* up the throat in a chain: the deep nest, then
Bourdons rooted up the shaft through [[The-Maw|the Maw]] and the dark above it, then surface
[[Chorister|Choristers]] feeding [[Cantor|Cantors]] out the lanes and [[Descant|Descants]] up to
the sky. While a Bourdon sings, the surface horde and the deep nest share **one set of eyes through
the ground**: a wave on the lanes attacks already knowing what's climbing the shaft beneath it, and
the deep sees the surface fight pressing down. Sever a Bourdon and you don't blind the source — it
is far too dense to sever, the way [[Perdition]] is — you **cut the column**: the stretch of
descent above the kill loses the deep nest's sight, and that segment falls feral, climbing into a
war it can no longer read.

## Behaviour

- **Deep-rooted, immobile.** Roots into the throat-wall of a descent shaft — fused rib-frame and
  horn-plate sunk into living stone — and never relocates; like the [[Chorister]] it is *planted*,
  just planted in the deep dark. It holds its station in the rock the way a [[Descant]] holds high
  station in the sky: depth is the whole point, and it never willingly climbs.
- While it lives, the Scourge above and below it on the shaft is **connected through the rock**:
  units on the surface and in the throat flank, focus-fire, and pre-empt movement as one mind
  ([[CANON]] §5), a pincer that reads the descent top to bottom. The bubble is *vertical and narrow*
  — it reaches up and down the column of stone, not out across it.
- Barely attacks. Its only direct threat is a slow subsonic resonance toll that staggers attackers
  and briefly scrambles their footing and targeting in the dark — a tremor you feel through the
  span before the body even comes into the light. It is defended *by* the horde climbing past it.
- **On death the column is severed.** Every connected Scourge on that stretch of the descent drops
  to feral ([[CANON]] §6): the climbers above lose the deep nest's sight, flanking collapses,
  the pincer comes apart. Smaller than severing a whole surface front — you blind a segment of
  shaft, not a breach — but it cracks the descent open one link at a time.
- Defended fanatically, the way the Scourge guards any relay — it spends bodies up the walls it
  would never otherwise burn to keep one Bourdon tolling, because losing one blinds the descent
  *from below* and hands a Purger a window in the worst place to need one.
- Killing a Bourdon does **not** kill the Scourge ([[CANON]] §2). The Choir persists; the deep nest
  grows a new Bourdon into the next bend of the shaft and the column re-roots in the rock overhead.

## Gameplay Read

- A **deep priority target and gate** — the subterranean counterpart to the surface
  [[Chorister]] objective, met on the way *down* rather than across a lane. The skill check is
  *reaching* it: it sits in the throat behind the climbing swarm, and the correct play is to fight
  down through the coordinated horde to the relay, then survive the feral remnant on a span over the
  dark.
- Telegraph: a slow toxic-green resonance toll pulses *up* the shaft-wall like a struck bell, and
  the climbers above and below flinch and snap into sync with each beat — the same "these are
  connected" grammar as the [[Chorister]], [[Cantor]], and [[Descant]], staged on the vertical-down
  axis. You feel the toll in the rock — screen-shake and a low sub-bass — before you see the body.
- The instant it dies, the stretch of descent above it visibly comes apart: climbers lose their
  footing-sync, the pincer slackens, the swarm on the walls stops leading you. The severance reads
  at a glance, no UI needed — the deep goes quiet under your boots.
- Frames the descent's connective payoff: breaking a Bourdon in the shaft is *why* a contested
  [[The-Hollow-Lanes|lane]] far overhead suddenly stops seeing a [[The-Wardens|Warden]] coming. The
  surface front and the deep nest are one Choir, climbing the same rock — and this is the node that
  proves it from below, exactly as a [[Descant]] proves it from above.

## Visual Motifs

- A vast rooted relay grown into **living rock** — a [[Scourge-Host-Families|Bone Titan Host]] of
  fused rib-cage, horn-plate, and massive buried limb-frames sunk into the throat-wall and
  overwritten by parasite tissue; clearly something *planted in stone*, half-swallowed by the
  shaft, not something that walks.
- Clustered resonator membranes and faceless droning throat-sacs packed deep in its frame — the
  [[Chorister]]'s singing organs re-grown for the low end — open, wet, swollen, pulsing slow and
  heavy in time with the toll.
- Toxic-green Choir nodes laddered up the buried spine, brightest at the crown breaking out of the
  rock-face; the up-facing emitter, the obvious "where do I shoot" answer in the dark.
- Tendrils and root-knots driving *down and out* into the stone — the column you can see, threading
  away into the rock toward the deep nest below rather than reaching across open ground.
- Rock-face overwritten by host-bone and parasite tissue, rupture seams bleeding green into the
  stone; the throat-wall of [[The-Maw|the Maw]] made flesh around it, warm and ribbed.

## Sprite Brief

- Views: front, side, back — readable in [[Scourge-Survivors]]'s close, dark FPS framing, lit
  mainly by its own crown-glow and muzzle flash.
- Silhouette must read as **half-buried, rooted-in-rock, and immense** — instantly distinct from the
  tall surface-mast [[Chorister]], the low scuttling [[Cantor]], and the wreckage-rooted [[Descant]].
- The crown node breaking out of the rock-face is the focal point: brightest up-facing Choir
  emitter, visible behind the climbing screen.
- Pulse state: a tolling variant with crown and embedded throat-sacs flared blood-hot-to-toxic and
  the rock-face around it visibly trembling.
- Sever state: a guttering variant — crown going dark, membranes slack, root-knots tearing loose
  from the stone — so the kill reads clearly in low light.

## Appears In

- [[Scourge-Survivors]] — the relay rooted in the throat-shaft on the descent past [[The-Maw|the
  Maw]] toward [[Perdition]]: while it tolls, the climbing horde fights as a coordinated pincer that
  reads the shaft top to bottom; sever it and that stretch of descent goes feral, buying the lone
  [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] Purger the window to push deeper. The deep counterpart to a surface
  [[Chorister]] objective.
- [[Deadlane]] — felt, not fought: while a Bourdon tolls in the rock beneath a held
  [[The-Hollow-Lanes|lane]], the surface wave arrives pre-aimed and coordinated with whatever is
  undermining the wall from below; a [[The-Wardens|Warden]] who breaches down and breaks it cuts the
  pincer and the lane stops seeing the player coming. The downward mirror of a [[Descant]]'s
  overhead handoff.
- [[The-Listeners]] — the deepest ear on the Choir, and nearly the hardest to reach. A Bourdon taken
  intact would be the heresy's view of the signal *closest to the source* ([[CANON]] §8), down by
  [[CANON|the Choir Node]] itself — coveted, almost never won, because the descent it sits in is the
  one no one walks out of.
