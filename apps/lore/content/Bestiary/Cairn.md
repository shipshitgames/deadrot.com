# Cairn

**At a glance:** [[Scourge-Host-Families|Bone Titan Host]] heavy / miniboss · slow walking wall the swarm shelters behind · enormous frontal armour, marrow-node weak points · ribcage reliquary disgorges a packed swarm on death · appears in [[Deadlane]] / [[Scourge-Survivors]].

The [[Scourge]] does not bury its dead — it spends them again. A Cairn is what a front looks like
after months of consumption, stacked upright and made to walk: a [[Scourge-Host-Families|Bone Titan Host]]
built not from one conquered megafauna but from *all of them*, stripped skeletons and battlefield
bone lashed into a single towering frame by parasite sinew. It is the locust math of [[CANON]] §2
made into a body — every host this swarm ate on this ground, given one last use. Where a [[Render]]
is what the Scourge makes of a soldier, a Cairn is what it makes of a graveyard.

A [[The-Wardens|Warden]] gunner sees it before anything else in the wave: a slow grey monument
rising out of the [[Cosmology|breach]] smoke, taller than the wall, its ribs caged around something
that glows. They do not waste the opening volley on it. Everyone who has held a lane knows the same
thing — the Cairn is not the threat, the Cairn is the *shadow the threat walks in*, and the worst
moment of the fight is the one right after you finally bring it down.

## Behaviour

- **Slow, towering, front-of-wave.** It leads heavy pushes at a funeral pace and never flanks. Its
  job is to *arrive*, not to chase. Swarm units route into its shadow and advance behind its bulk,
  using it as moving cover from tower fire and ranged defenders.
- **A damage sponge, by design.** Stacked bone-plate armours the front; it soaks punishment a whole
  wave's worth of [[Swarm-Ripper|Rippers]] would die to. Shooting the frame is mostly wasted ammo —
  the armour is the dead, and the dead don't mind being shot.
- **Weak points are the marrow.** Toxic-green parasite nodes glow at the joints and seams where the
  frame is lashed together, and a breach core sits deep in the ribcage reliquary. Hit the marrow,
  not the bone — the correct fight is precision into a slow target while its escort tries to bury you.
- Primary attack: a slow overhead sweep of a fused bone-club limb that shatters cover, barricades,
  and anything that stood still to line up a shot. Telegraphed and avoidable alone; lethal when the
  swarm has already pinned your footing.
- **On death the reliquary opens.** When the frame finally falls, the ribcage splits and disgorges
  the cluster of dormant smaller hosts packed inside it — a fresh, point-blank swarm dropped onto
  whoever just killed it. Bring a Cairn down in a corner or in melee and you are buried by its
  contents; bring it down with range and space and you control the second wave instead.
- **Not a relay.** A Cairn carries no Choir node and does no coordinating of its own — its bulk and
  its escort are its whole threat. While its wave thinks as one mind, that is a nearby [[Chorister]]
  or [[Cantor]]'s doing ([[CANON]] §5–6), not the Cairn's; sever that node and the Cairn lumbers on
  *feral*, still a wall, but a blind and stupid one.
- Killing a Cairn does **not** cost the Scourge anything it values — it was already dead biomass
  spent forward ([[CANON]] §2). The hive builds another from the next month's harvest.

## Gameplay Read

- A **tank-and-trap**, not a pressure unit: the encounter is built around *how* you kill it, not
  whether you can. The skill check is positioning for the disgorge, plus precision onto the marrow.
- Telegraph: a grey monumental silhouette, far taller and slower than any other heavy; the
  toxic-green marrow-nodes at its joints and the core glow in its ribcage are the obvious "shoot
  here" answer against an otherwise dull-bone body.
- Readable death: the ribcage visibly cracks and swings open a beat before the packed swarm spills
  out — a clear, no-UI tell that the second wave is coming and from exactly where.
- Counter-play has a cost either way: kill it early at range and you eat the disgorge while the main
  wave is still closing; let it walk and it shields the whole push to your wall before it dies.

## Visual Motifs

- A walking tower of fused skeletons — megafauna ribs forming a cage, smaller skulls and stripped
  human and host dead packed into the frame, bone-pale and weathered, clearly *assembled* rather
  than grown.
- Parasite sinew and black chitin lashing the stack together at every joint, threading through eye
  sockets and marrow channels — the grammar that marks it Scourge and not a mere pile of bone.
- Toxic-green marrow glowing inside the bone at the lashing-points; a brighter breach core buried
  deep in the ribcage reliquary, half-hidden behind the ribs that cage it.
- The dormant swarm visible packed inside the ribcage — wet shapes folded into the dry frame, the
  threat you can see before it's loose.
- One oversized bone-club limb, a femur or tusk-mass fused with chitin; the opposite side a heaving
  knot of sinew holding the stack upright.

## Sprite Brief

- Views: front, side, back.
- Silhouette must read **tall, slow, and monumental** at lane distance — instantly distinct from the
  low charging [[Render]], the wide low [[Rot-Engine]], and the walker-framed [[Graft-Breacher]].
- The marrow-nodes and the caged ribcage core are the focal points: the "where do I shoot" answer is
  the glow inside the bone, not the bone itself.
- Death state: a ribcage-open variant, frame cracked wide with the packed swarm spilling out — the
  disgorge made readable.
- Back view: the load-bearing sinew knot — the most consumed, softest mass, the reward for flanking.
- Must stay readable in both [[Deadlane]]'s lane-top camera and [[Scourge-Survivors]]'s close FPS framing.

## Appears In

- [[Deadlane]] — a heavy-wave wall: it tanks tower fire and shields the swarm closing on the line,
  so a [[The-Wardens|Warden]] must choose between burning it down early (and eating the disgorge mid-lane)
  or letting it shepherd the push all the way to the wall. A prioritisation puzzle, not a damage race.
- [[Scourge-Survivors]] — a breach-room miniboss for a lone [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] Purger ([[Perdition]]):
  the room is the trap, because killing the Cairn floods it with the swarm it was carrying; the
  intended play is to break it from the doorway with room to fall back, not in the open.
