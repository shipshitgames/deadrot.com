# Cantor

**At a glance:** [[Scourge-Host-Families|Chitin Warhost]] mobile Choir-repeater · carries a Choir node on its back so a roaming pack stays one mind · cowardly runner that flees and dies its pack feral · appears in [[Deadlane]] / [[Scourge-Survivors]].

A mobile **Choir-repeater** — the second named relay class after the rooted [[Chorister]]. Where
a Chorister is *planted* astride a breach and never willingly moves, a Cantor **runs with the
swarm**: a low, fast [[Scourge-Host-Families|Chitin Warhost]] grown around a single Choir node it
carries on its back, so a roaming pack stays connected to the **Choir** ([[CANON]] §5–6) far past
the reach of any fixed relay. The Scourge cannot mass densely enough to think across the open
[[The-Hollow-Lanes|lanes]] on its own; a Chorister extends the signal's *radius*, but a Cantor
extends its *range of motion*. It is how the horde keeps one set of eyes while it pushes deep
into ground a stationary node could never hold.

Kill the Cantor and you sever just its pack — a smaller, sharper, more frequent version of the
Chorister's severance. The swarm it was carrying drops to feral ([[CANON]] §6): still lethal,
but blind, dumb, instinct-only. You do not blind a whole front the way you do by breaking a
Chorister; you blind the squad in front of you, right now.

## Behaviour

- **Mobile, not rooted.** Keeps pace just behind the leading [[Swarm-Ripper|Rippers]], threading
  through the pack so the bodies it empowers are also the bodies that screen it. It never leads a
  charge and never holds ground — it follows the swarm's center of mass and flees danger.
- While it lives, every Scourge in its (smaller) radius is **connected**: the pack flanks,
  focus-fires, and pre-empts movement as one mind, exactly like a Chorister's horde — but the
  bubble travels *with* the Cantor, so the coordination chases you through a lane instead of
  sitting on a fixed breach.
- Barely attacks. Its only direct threat is a short scuttling lunge and a close-range resonance
  snap that briefly scrambles attacker targeting — enough to buy a half-second to bolt back into
  the pack.
- **On death its pack is severed.** Every connected Scourge in its travelling radius drops to
  feral ([[CANON]] §6): tracking goes loose, flanking collapses, the squad scatters. Smaller and
  more local than a Chorister break, and it re-forms the moment the next Cantor arrives.
- Self-preserving to the point of cowardice — the only Scourge unit that reliably *retreats*. The
  hive spends Rippers to keep a Cantor alive and moving, because losing one blinds a spearhead
  mid-push, but it will pull a Cantor out of a fight a Chorister would die rooted in.
- Killing a Cantor does **not** kill the Scourge ([[CANON]] §2). The Choir persists; another
  Cantor peels off the nearest [[Chorister]]'s node-cluster and runs the signal forward again.

## Gameplay Read

- A **mobile priority target** — the moving-objective counterpart to the Chorister's fixed one.
  The skill check is *catching* it: it hides in the crowd it coordinates and bolts when exposed.
- Telegraph: a tight toxic-green resonance pulse beats out from the Cantor's back-node, and the
  units around it flinch and snap in sync with each beat — the same "these are connected" grammar
  as the Chorister, but on a bubble you have to track through the fight.
- The instant it dies, the pack it was carrying visibly goes loose — the readable severance, no UI
  needed — then re-tightens if another Cantor reaches the same ground.
- Rewards aggression and target ID over patience: the correct play is to push *into* the
  coordinated pack to reach the runner, not to wait it out, because waiting just lets it carry the
  Choir deeper down the lane.

## Visual Motifs

- A low, fast multi-legged chitin host — scuttling, hunched, built to *keep up*, never upright or
  rooted. Reads as a courier, not a fighter.
- A single oversized Choir node fused into a humped carapace on its back — the brightest
  toxic-green point on the body, the obvious "where do I shoot" answer, raised and exposed by the
  carrying posture.
- Resonator membranes packed along the flanks beneath the hump, wet and pulsing in time with the
  broadcast — the [[Chorister]]'s singing throat-sacs, shrunk and made portable.
- Short tendrils trailing from the node, snapping out to lash into nearby host bodies as it runs —
  the network you can see, made mobile.
- Cracked alien carapace rebuilt by parasite tissue, black chitin and rupture seams; the legs are
  the most intact part, the back the most consumed — built around protecting and carrying the node.

## Sprite Brief

- Views: front, side, back.
- Silhouette must read as **low and fast with a raised back-node** — instantly distinct from the
  tall, rooted, mast-like [[Chorister]] and from a plain charger like the [[Render]].
- The back-node hump is the focal point: bright Choir node, the shoot-here answer, visible even
  when the body is screened by the pack.
- Pulse state: an emitting variant with the back-node and flank membranes flared blood-hot-to-toxic
  and trailing tendrils taut.
- Flee state: a scuttling-away variant — legs splayed for speed, node clamped low, reading clearly
  as "it's running."
- Must stay readable in both [[Deadlane]]'s lane-top camera and [[Scourge-Survivors]]'s close FPS
  framing, even mid-pack.
