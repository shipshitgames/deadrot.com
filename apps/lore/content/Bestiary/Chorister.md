# Chorister

**At a glance:** [[Scourge]] rooted Choir-repeater · [[Scourge-Host-Families|Mycelial Spore Host]] relay that extends the Choir past its natural radius · priority objective; destroy it and the local swarm goes feral · appears in [[Deadlane]] / [[Scourge-Survivors]] / [[The-Listeners]].

A named **Choir-repeater** — the physical node the Scourge grows to carry its hive signal past
its natural radius. It does not fight so much as *sing*: a rooted, throat-heavy relay organism
that broadcasts the **Choir** ([[CANON]] §5–6) to every Scourge around it, so the horde in its
range stays coordinated, near-omniscient, and sharing one set of eyes. The Scourge cannot mass
densely enough to think across a whole front on its own — Choristers are how the connection
*reaches*. Kill the Chorister and you kill the signal: the local node is **severed from the
Choir** and the swarm around it goes feral. This is the human win condition made flesh.

## Behaviour

- Semi-stationary. Roots into the ground near a [[Cosmology|breach]] or astride a lane and
  anchors there; relocates only slowly, dragging its tendrils, and never willingly.
- While it lives, every Scourge in its radius is **connected**: units flank, focus-fire,
  pre-empt the player's movement, and react as one mind. Pull a [[Swarm-Ripper|Ripper]] out of
  cover and three more cover the angle. The Chorister is *why*.
- Barely attacks on its own — it is defended *by* the horde it empowers. Its only direct threat
  is a close-range resonance burst that staggers and briefly scrambles attacker targeting.
- **On death the local node is severed.** Every connected Scourge in range drops to feral
  ([[CANON]] §6): still lethal, but blind, dumb, instinct-only — no shared sight, no
  coordination. The swarm scatters from a tide into a panic.
- The Scourge defends a Chorister fanatically; it will throw bodies it would never otherwise
  spend to keep one singing. Losing one blinds an entire front, so it never gives one up cheap.
- Killing a Chorister does **not** kill the Scourge — the wider Choir persists and re-roots
  elsewhere ([[CANON]] §2). You sever a node and win the fight; you do not win the war.

## Gameplay Read

- A **priority target and objective**, not a pressure unit — the thing the whole encounter is
  built around reaching and breaking.
- Telegraph: a slow toxic-green resonance pulse radiates out from the Chorister, and you can
  *see* nearby units flinch and snap in sync with each beat — the visual grammar of "these are
  connected."
- The moment it dies, the surrounding horde visibly changes behaviour: tracking goes loose,
  units wander, flanking collapses. The severance is readable at a glance, no UI needed.
- Heavily screened; the intended challenge is fighting *through* the coordinated swarm to the
  relay, then surviving the feral remnant.

## Visual Motifs

- A rooted relay-organism grown tall — a fused mast of bone, black chitin, and stretched host
  cartilage, more **[[Scourge-Host-Families|Mycelial Spore Host]]** than warrior: fibrous,
  swollen, breathing.
- Clustered throat-sacs and resonator membranes along its length — open, wet, *singing* mouths
  with no faces, pulsing in time with the broadcast.
- Toxic-green Choir nodes laddered up the spine, brightest at the crown; the signal-emitter.
- Mycelial filaments and tendrils webbing out from its base across the ground, threading into
  every host body nearby — the network you can literally see.
- Anchored into a breach core or the earth by a knot of roots; clearly something that was
  *planted*, not something that walks.

## Sprite Brief

- Views: front, side, back.
- Reads as a structure/relay even at lane distance — tall, rooted, asymmetric; never mistakable
  for an infantry charger.
- Crown and throat-sacs are the focal point: the "where do I shoot" answer is the brightest
  Choir node.
- Pulse state: an emitting variant with crown and sacs flared blood-hot-to-toxic, tendrils taut.
- Must read in both [[Deadlane]]'s lane-top camera and [[Scourge-Survivors]]'s close FPS framing.

## Appears In

- [[Deadlane]] — a relay planted on a held lane: while it sings, waves attack as a coordinated
  push; destroy it and the lane's Scourge falls into feral disorder. A [[The-Wardens|Warden]]
  objective and turning point.
- [[Scourge-Survivors]] — the **Purge** objective deep in a breach ([[Perdition]]): a lone
  [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] Purger fights through the connected swarm to sever the node from the inside.
- [[The-Listeners]] — the organ the heresy covets. A severed-but-living Chorister is exactly
  the node they keep alive and *listen* to ([[CANON]] §8) — the closest thing to a captured ear
  on the Choir.
