# Descant

An **orbital Choir-repeater** — the third named relay class after the rooted [[Chorister]] and the
mobile [[Cantor]], and the one that holds the **Choir** ([[CANON]] §5–6) up the *gravity well*. A
Chorister extends the signal's **radius** across a breach; a Cantor extends its **range of motion**
along a lane; a Descant extends it **vertically**, stitching the Scourge falling through orbit
([[Starblight]]) to the Choir already rooted on the ground. It is a [[Scourge-Host-Families|Voidship
Host]] grown into captured orbital wreckage — a relay holding high station, singing *down* the climb.

The Choir's radius is too short to reach orbit on its own; the connection has to be *carried* up the
column in a chain — surface [[Chorister|Choristers]], then atmospheric relays, then a Descant riding
high station as the apex node. While a Descant sings, the orbital Scourge and the surface horde share
**one set of eyes across the whole front, top to bottom**: incoming breach-ships ([[Orbital-Breach-Carrier]])
descend already knowing where the lanes are thin, and the ground sees the sky fall before it lands.
Sever the Descant and you don't just blind a breach or a pack — you **cut the sky off from the
ground**. The orbital Scourge keeps arriving, but it arrives *blind*: feral on descent, uncoordinated
with the nests below, falling into a war it can no longer read.

## Behaviour

- **High-station, barely mobile.** Holds an orbital or high-atmosphere anchor — rooted into a dead
  station, a gutted hull, or the spine of a spent breach-ship — and station-keeps with slow tendril
  thrust. Like the [[Chorister]] it is *planted*, just planted in the sky; it never willingly drops
  its altitude, because altitude is the whole point.
- While it lives, the orbital Scourge in its column is **connected to the surface Choir**: spore
  clouds, [[Scourge-Fighter|fighters]], and carriers coordinate with the ground horde and with each
  other, threading attack patterns as one mind ([[CANON]] §5). The bubble is *tall*, not wide — it
  reaches down the gravity well, not out across orbit.
- Barely attacks. Its only direct threat is a long, slow resonance lance that scrambles a pilot's
  targeting on a near pass — enough to break a firing run, not to kill. It is defended *by* the craft
  it empowers, screened behind escorting fighters and spore mines.
- **On death the column is severed.** Every connected Scourge falling through that orbital lane drops
  to feral ([[CANON]] §6): incoming breach-ships arrive blind and instinct-only, no shared sight with
  the ground, no pre-empting the lanes below. The sky stops being coordinated with the surface.
- Defended fanatically, the way the Scourge defends any relay — it spends [[Scourge-Fighter|fighters]]
  it would never otherwise burn to keep one Descant singing, because losing one blinds an entire front
  *from above* and hands the ground war a window it can feel.
- Killing a Descant does **not** kill the Scourge ([[CANON]] §2). The Choir persists; the next breach-ship
  to make high station grows a new Descant from its hull, and the column re-roots overhead.

## Gameplay Read

- A **high-value priority target** for [[Starblight]] — the orbital counterpart to the ground Purge
  objective. The skill check is *reaching* it: it sits deep behind escort formations and bullet-heaven
  patterns, and the correct play is to thread the screen to the relay, not to attrition the swarm.
- Telegraph: a slow toxic-green resonance pulse falls *downward* from the Descant like a dropped curtain,
  and the craft beneath it flinch and snap into sync with each beat — the same "these are connected"
  grammar as the [[Chorister]] and [[Cantor]], staged on the vertical instead of the horizontal.
- The instant it dies, the incoming wave visibly comes apart: fighters break formation, breach-ships
  drift off their descent lines, spore mines stop leading the player. The severance reads at a glance,
  no UI needed — the sky goes dumb.
- Frames the connective payoff: breaking a Descant in [[Starblight]] is *why* a lane in [[Deadlane]]
  suddenly stops seeing the player coming. The orbital front and the surface front are one Choir, and
  this is the node that proves it.

## Visual Motifs

- A rooted relay grown into **orbital wreckage** — a fused mast of black chitin and stretched host
  cartilage threaded through a gutted hull, dead engine bells, and snapped antenna booms; clearly a
  [[Scourge-Host-Families|Voidship Host]], something that was *planted in a ship*, not something that flies.
- Clustered resonator membranes and faceless singing throat-sacs packed down one flank — the
  [[Chorister]]'s organs, re-grown for vacuum — open, wet, pulsing in time with the broadcast.
- Toxic-green Choir nodes laddered along the spine, brightest at the crown; the down-facing emitter, the
  obvious "where do I shoot" answer.
- Tendrils and cabling trailing *downward* off the anchor — the column you can see, reaching for the
  ground far below rather than out across the dark.
- Anchored into a dead station ring or breach-ship spine by a knot of roots and fused hull plate; rusted
  metal overwritten by parasite tissue, rupture seams bleeding green.

## Sprite Brief

- Views: front, side, back — readable at [[Starblight]]'s arcade scale, screened mid-formation.
- Silhouette must read as **tall and rooted-in-wreckage**, station-keeping — instantly distinct from the
  agile [[Scourge-Fighter]] and from the bulk of the [[Orbital-Breach-Carrier]] boss.
- The crown node is the focal point: brightest down-facing Choir emitter, visible behind escorts.
- Pulse state: an emitting variant with crown and throat-sacs flared blood-hot-to-toxic and the downward
  tendril-column taut.
- Sever state: a guttering variant — crown going dark, membranes slack, the column snapping loose — so
  the kill reads clearly even at speed.

## Appears In

- [[Starblight]] — the orbital relay holding high station so incoming breach matter descends coordinated
  with the ground; a [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] or [[The-Wardens|Warden]] pilot must thread the escort screen and
  break it to blind the falling wave. The high-value priority target between the [[Scourge-Fighter]]
  swarm and the [[Orbital-Breach-Carrier]] boss.
- [[Deadlane]] — felt, not fought: while a Descant sings overhead, lane waves arrive pre-aimed and ahead
  of the player's read; the moment orbit severs it, the surface horde loses its top-down sight and a
  contested [[The-Hollow-Lanes|lane]] becomes survivable. The clearest cross-front handoff in the canon.
- [[The-Listeners]] — the highest ear on the Choir, and the hardest to reach. A Descant taken intact would
  be the heresy's view of the signal *across the whole gravity well* ([[CANON]] §8) — coveted, almost
  never won, because the Scourge dies in orbit defending it.
