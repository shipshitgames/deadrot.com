# Graft-Breacher

**At a glance:** [[Scourge-Host-Families|Machine-Graft]] elite melee wrecker · slow armored siege engine that ignores infantry and shears through towers and walls · soft parasite back rewards flanking fire · appears in [[Deadlane]] / [[Scourge-Survivors]] / [[Rothulk]].

A [[Scourge-Host-Families|Machine-Graft]] [[Scourge]] elite grown through the frame of a
conquered industrial machine — pistons, hull plating, and hydraulic limbs rewired by parasite
tissue into a living siege engine. Its original function is irretrievable; what remains is a
slow, armoured **melee wrecker** that ignores soft targets entirely and tears through towers,
walls, and barricades until nothing stands. It is the close-range counterpart to the
[[Rot-Engine]]: where the Rot-Engine *dissolves* a fortification from range, the Graft-Breacher
walks up and *shears it apart*.

## Behaviour

- Advances at a methodical pace; swarm units route around it to fill the gap it punches open.
- Prioritises structures over infantry — towers, walls, and barricades draw aggression first.
- Primary attack: a massive demolition-claw (the machine's original heavy arm, now fused
  with chitin and parasite sinew) that shears through fortified material in two or three swings.
- Secondary: when the demolition arm is destroyed or the Graft-Breacher takes sustained
  flanking fire, the exposed parasite mass erupts — a tendril burst that staggers nearby
  infantry and briefly blinds tower targeting.
- Heavily armoured from the front; the parasite growth crowding the back plates is softer
  and takes bonus damage, rewarding flanking fire.

## Gameplay Read

- Large, slow silhouette — reads as a structure threat even at full lane distance.
- Industrial frame (riveted hull, pistons, hydraulic joints) still recognisable through the
  parasite overgrowth.
- One oversized demolition-claw on the dominant arm; the opposite limb is a wet tendril cluster.
- Toxic-green breach cores glow from the torso where the machine's control systems used to be.
- Taking sustained fire from behind triggers a visible tendril-burst tell before the eruption.

## Visual Motifs

- Heavy machine frame consumed from within — metal seams split by chitin growth, sinew
  threading through piston rods.
- Black chitin crawling up hydraulic joints and riveted hull plates.
- Toxic-green nodes at former gauge panels and control points.
- Asymmetry: one massive machine-arm preserved as a siege weapon; the opposite side replaced
  by a heaving mass of wet parasite tissue and reaching tendrils.
- Rust, cracked industrial hull paint, and old warning-stripe markings barely legible under
  the growth.
