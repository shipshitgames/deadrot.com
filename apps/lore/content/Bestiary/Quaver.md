# Quaver

The first repeater a Pyre Purger ever kills is usually the wrong one. You go in low through a
breach, you find the rooted thing singing in the dark, you put it down — and the swarm doesn't
break. It keeps flanking. Keeps pre-empting. Keeps one set of eyes on you. Then you look up.
There's a winged host turning slow circles two body-lengths above the horde, ribcage hollowed
into a sounding-box, throat lit green, and it has been watching you the whole time through
everyone else's eyes.

That's a Quaver. It isn't an elite. It's a grunt the Choir grew wings and a resonator onto and
threw into the air, and the Scourge will spend a hundred of them on a bad afternoon without
flinching — because a relay that flies is a relay you lose. It never holds still: it wavers and
bobs on its own updraft, trembling at the top of every wingbeat, the motion the old-world
musicians named it for. It keeps a careful gap and spits green from above while the horde
screens its body. Hit it once and it folds like wet paper. The whole trick is having a barrel
free when you finally notice it.

**At a glance:** Flesh-host **Choir-repeater**, the airborne/mobile relay class · fast, fragile,
hovers out of melee reach and spits at range · keeps a local pocket of swarm coordinated where
no rooted node stands — kill it and that pocket drops feral · the Choir's cheap, expendable
sky-ear, spent like ammunition · appears in [[Scourge-Survivors]] / [[Deadlane]].

## Behaviour

- **Airborne and restless.** Hovers about two body-lengths up, out of melee reach, on a
  trembling bob; never roots, never holds a spot for long. A mobile relay has to keep moving to
  keep the net stitched over ground the swarm hasn't filled.
- **Holds the gap and spits.** Keeps its distance and lobs toxic-green signal-bolts — a chip of
  the Choir's own voice spat as a weapon. Backs off and holds fire rather than let anything
  close; the horde underneath is its wall.
- **Screened, not armored.** Light and fragile. It flinches hard off a single hit and a solid
  burst drops it — staggering one cuts the signal mid-note. It survives by altitude and by the
  bodies below it, never by toughness.
- **A mobile mini-repeater, not an anchor.** While it flies over a patch of swarm, the locals
  stay connected to the Choir through it even with no rooted [[Chorister]] in range. It does not
  sever a *front* the way killing a Chorister does — it patches gaps: over open killing-ground,
  during a push, in the seconds before a node roots.
- **Kill it and the pocket drops.** Every Scourge it was carrying in that patch goes briefly
  **feral** ([[CANON]] §6) — loose, blind, instinct-only — until another Quaver drifts over or a
  rooted relay covers the gap. A soft, local, temporary version of the Chorister severance.
- **Spent freely.** Killing one doesn't dent the wider Choir ([[CANON]] §2); the Scourge simply
  lofts another. It grows them off cheap flesh stock and treats them like ammunition.

## Gameplay Read

- A **soft-priority target**: not a pressure unit you must kill to live, but the reason the
  horde around you feels *smart*. Drop it and the nearby swarm gets dumber for a few seconds —
  tracking goes loose, flanking sags.
- **Lofted once a front matures.** You won't see them in the opening push; they thicken the
  longer a run survives or a lane holds.
- **Pays out for the awkward shot.** Out of melee reach and hiding behind altitude, it taxes
  your ranged answer and your aim-up. Worth the most XP of the common hosts because it's the
  hardest of them to punish.
- **Telegraph:** a green throat-flare before each spit, and the bob steadies for half a beat as
  it sounds. Heavily screened — the challenge is carving a firing line up through the horde.

## Visual Motifs

- A gaunt flesh-host — the same blood-and-rust [[Scourge-Host-Families|Rot-Infested Flesh Host]]
  grammar as the swarm below — given grown flight membranes: broad, ribbed, leathery wings
  stretched over parasite cartilage. Nothing clean about them.
- The relay organ is its **own ribcage, hollowed and re-strung into a sounding-box** — open
  between the ribs, resonator membranes stretched across the gap, breathing.
- A toxic-green Choir node at the throat and a horned crown, brightest when it spits; the
  emitter and the only clean color on it.
- Talons and a ruined, hanging lower body that never touches ground — clearly something that
  was *lifted*, not something that walks.

## Sprite Brief

- Views: front, side, back.
- Reads as airborne and fragile at a glance — wings spread, body small and high; never
  mistakable for a grounded charger.
- Hover-bob plus a trembling wingbeat in motion; a steadied half-beat on the spit telegraph.
- Throat/crown node is the focal point and the brightest pixel; flares on fire.
- Must read at [[Scourge-Survivors]]'s close framing and [[Deadlane]]'s lane-top camera; small
  silhouette, clear wing read.

## Prompt Seed

Scourge Choir-repeater, airborne flesh-host relay: gaunt blood-red parasite body with broad
ribbed leathery wings grown on raw cartilage, hollowed ribcage re-strung into a resonating
sounding-box with stretched membranes, toxic-green Choir node glowing at throat and horned
crown, taloned ruined lower body hanging, hovering two body-lengths over a horde and spitting a
green signal-bolt, blood and rust and gunmetal, DOOM-like heavy shadows, no neon, no clean
sci-fi.
