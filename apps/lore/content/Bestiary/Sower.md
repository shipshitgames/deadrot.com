# Sower

**At a glance:** [[Scourge-Host-Families|Rot-Infested Flesh Host]] elite · the infester — converts the fresh dead and downed into feral swarm mid-fight · barely fights, harvests · counter is **starvation**: deny it bodies ([[CANON]] §6) · keeps seeding even when severed and feral · appears in [[Scourge-Survivors]] / [[Deadlane]] / [[Perdition]].

Every other unit on a front is something the [[Scourge]] already *made*. A Sower is the Scourge **making more**. It is the single body on the field that performs the act every locked fact rests on — a **host-dependent parasite** that *wears whatever it infects* ([[CANON]] §1), shown not as a finished monster but as the moment of taking. Where a [[Cairn]] stacks dry, spent bone into a wall, a Sower wants the *soft* dead: the [[The-Wardens|Warden]] who fell ten seconds ago, the gut-shot survivor still crawling, the body whose warmth is the only thing the parasite needs. It crouches over them, drives a seed-quill home, and what gets back up is feral [[Swarm-Ripper|swarm]] wearing a face the line knew by name.

This is the horror [[CANON]] keeps sharpest made into a behaviour: **you cannot negotiate with need**. The Sower has no malice and no plan — it is not desecrating your dead to spite you, it simply cannot tell the difference between a corpse and a resource, because for the parasite there is none ([[CANON]] §1). It is the locust math of [[CANON]] §2 closing its loop in real time: the war eats biomass, and the Sower turns the war's leavings straight back into more mouths. A holdout learns fast that the worst place to lose someone is in a Sower's reach, and that the cruelest thing the Scourge does is also the most efficient — it makes you fight your own dead before they've cooled.

## Behaviour

- **A harvester, not a fighter.** It trails the wave, never leads it, and ignores live, upright threats almost entirely — it moves toward *bodies*: the freshly killed, the downed, the dying. Its whole tropism is the harvest behind the fight, not the fight itself.
- **Conversion is its only real attack.** Reaching a viable host, it pins the body and drives a **seed-quill** in; after a short, interruptible channel the corpse spasms and rises as a fresh **feral** [[Swarm-Ripper|Ripper]] — blind, dumb, instinct-only ([[CANON]] §6), thrown straight back into the line. A Sower left alone in a kill-zone refills the wave it walks behind from the wave you just dropped.
- **Bodies are the resource — so deny it bodies.** It is the [[Scourge]]'s reproduction shown as a unit, and therefore the **starvation** weakness ([[CANON]] §6) made literal: burn or break a corpse before the quill lands and the Sower has nothing to take; clear the field and it starves with the rest of the horde.
- **It seeds even severed.** Cut the local [[Chorister]] or [[Cantor]] and the Sower drops feral with everything else — and *keeps converting anyway*, blind and uncoordinated, because seeding isn't a tactic it was told to run, it's the one thing the parasite does with or without the Choir. The purest image in the bestiary of need with no off-switch ([[CANON]] §1).
- **Barely defends itself.** Its only direct threat is a close-range spray of the same seed-fluid that briefly blinds and corrodes — enough to buy a step back to the next body. It survives by being screened by the swarm it replenishes, not by fighting.
- **Self-preserving, like the relays.** The hive spends [[Swarm-Ripper|Rippers]] to keep a Sower working a field it would never otherwise burn, because a live Sower turns your kills into its reinforcements; it pulls one out of a losing fight the way it never would a charger.
- Killing a Sower does **not** kill the Scourge ([[CANON]] §2). The Choir persists and grows another; you have only stopped *this* field from being turned against you, *this* time.

## Gameplay Read

- A **tempo target and a moral one** — not a damage threat but a force-multiplier on the dead. The skill check is *triage under fire*: do you push the swarm, or peel off to deny the Sower the bodies it's about to draft? Ignore it and the encounter never drains, because your kills become its spawns.
- Telegraph: it reads as wrong on sight — upright but **wretched**, hands-first, moving *against* the wave's flow toward the fallen instead of with it toward you. The seed-quill arm and the wet ovipositor-sac at its belly are the obvious "shoot here," brightest mid-channel.
- Readable conversion: a crouched channel over a body with a rising toxic-green glow at the quill, a clear beat where the kill is interruptible — drop the Sower or destroy the corpse before the glow peaks and the host stays dead; miss the window and it stands back up as feral swarm.
- Counter-play has a cost: chasing the Sower pulls you off the line and into the harvest-ground behind the wave, exactly where your own dead are; the [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] answer — *burn the bodies so nothing can take them* — denies the Sower but spends time, ammo, and the ground itself.

## Visual Motifs

- A hunched, hands-first [[Scourge-Host-Families|Rot-Infested Flesh Host]] — humanoid stock split open by parasite growth, ruined armour and a half-recognisable face still on it, built to *kneel and work* over a body rather than stand and fight. The most human-shaped horror on the field, which is the point.
- A long bone **seed-quill** grown from one forearm — a hollow ovipositor-lance, wet at the tip, that drives into a host; the opposite hand a cluster of grasping, sorting tendrils for pinning the body.
- A swollen, semi-transparent **brood-sac** slung at the belly, packed with toxic-green seed visibly draining down the quill arm with each conversion — the resource, and the obvious weak point, brightest when it's seeding.
- Rupture seams weeping seed-fluid; tendrils trailing from the sac to whatever it last touched, threading the harvest behind it into the swarm ahead.
- Around a worked field: the half-turned — bodies in mid-conversion, seam-split and glowing at the quill-wound, the threat you can see being *made*.

## Sprite Brief

- Views: front, side, back.
- Silhouette must read as **upright, hunched, hands-first, and wretched** — instantly distinct from the low charging [[Render]], the wide [[Rot-Engine]], and the tall mast-like [[Chorister]]; it should read as "wrong-human," not "soldier."
- The brood-sac and seed-quill are the focal points: the "where do I shoot" answer is the glowing sac at the belly and the wet quill-tip, brightest mid-conversion.
- Seeding state: a crouched-over-a-body variant, quill driven home, sac and quill flared blood-hot-to-toxic — the interruptible channel made readable.
- Back view: the brood-sac's anchor and the trailing tendrils — the most consumed, softest mass, the reward for flanking before it can seed again.
- Must stay readable in both [[Deadlane]]'s lane-top camera and [[Scourge-Survivors]]'s close FPS framing.

## Appears In

- [[Scourge-Survivors]] — the unit that makes a breach-room ([[Perdition]]) *refuse to empty*: while a Sower works the floor, the lone [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] Purger's kills get back up as feral swarm behind them, so the correct play is to hunt the Sower or burn the dead, not to out-shoot a room that reloads itself from your own casualties.
- [[Deadlane]] — trails the wave into the kill-zone in front of a [[The-Wardens|Warden]] line and drafts the fallen — Scourge and defenders alike — into fresh feral [[Swarm-Ripper|Rippers]] mid-push; a Warden who lets a Sower reach the bodies piling at the wall finds the wave refilling from the inside, so it becomes a priority kill the moment it's flagged.
- [[The-Listeners]] — proof, in flesh, of the heresy's whole thesis ([[CANON]] §8): a Sower is the Scourge running its one imperative — *take a host, make more* — with no plan, no hate, and no off-switch, severed or connected alike. The [[The-Listeners|Listeners]] point to it and argue you do not *reason* with that; you *redirect* it. Pyre and Warden alike answer the same way they answer the parasite — with fire.
