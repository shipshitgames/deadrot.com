# Spore-Casket

**At a glance:** [[Scourge-Host-Families|Voidship Host]] living spore mine - a coffin of infected hull, mycelial slurry, and stolen corpse-seed that drifts through [[The-Skyhook|Skyhook]] debris fields - not a [[CANON|Choir]] repeater or a breach; it is physical host payload waiting to burst - counter is distance, flak, fire, and clean burn vectors - appears in [[Starblight]] / [[Warline]], with its first sighting in [[Zero-Day]].

The first rule in orbit is never chase the quiet ones.

A [[Scourge-Fighter]] dives. An [[Orbital-Breach-Carrier]] opens like a wound and dares the whole gunline to look at it. A Spore-Casket does less. It tumbles cold through wreckage, half coffin and half mine, its hull dark enough to vanish against the void until the pilot's lamp catches bone-white growth under the rust. Then the thing twitches, corrects by a meter, and starts counting down with a green pulse behind the plates.

It is not a breach. It is not a singer. It is not a clever little ship pretending to be dead.

It is a delivery body.

The [[Scourge]] grows Spore-Caskets out of whatever orbit gives it: spent escape pods, missile casings, broken drone shells, sealed cargo coffins, frozen casualties, and sloughed breach tissue packed tight around mycelial host-slurry. While the [[CANON|Choir]] reaches it through a [[Descant]] or carrier column, the casket drifts with ugly purpose. It slides into intercept lanes, hides inside debris, and waits until a pilot, shield line, or falling transport comes close enough to matter. Sever the column and the casket goes feral with everything else. It still drifts. It still bursts. It only stops leading the target.

That is the Skyhook's arithmetic: every casket killed high is one nest that does not get the chance to become ground work.

When it opens, it opens wrong. The outer hull cracks into petal-shards. Tendons snap taut between metal ribs. Spores, acid frost, parasite hooks, and tiny crawling seed-hosts scatter in a cone. Against a fighter, it is a proximity mine. Against the [[The-Skyhook|Skyhook]], it is contamination trying to bite into panels, tether joints, fuel skin, and people. Against the planet below, it is a little future breach that failed to become famous.

The [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] burn them on sight. The [[The-Wardens|Wardens]] mark fields of them and make pilots clear vectors one slow pass at a time. Both doctrines agree on the only part that matters: do not let the coffin decide where it dies.

## Behaviour

- **Drifts as payload, not command.** A Spore-Casket carries host seed, spore acid, and parasite hooks. It does not extend the Choir, relay orders, or keep other Scourge coordinated. A nearby [[Descant]] or [[Orbital-Breach-Carrier]] may guide it; the casket itself is not a node.
- **Hides in orbital clutter.** It masks inside wreckage, dead pods, spent munitions, and frozen debris around [[The-Skyhook]]. Connected caskets lead targets and pressure flight lanes; severed caskets coast on instinct and proximity.
- **Bursts on closeness or damage.** If a ship crosses its kill bubble, the casket blossoms into hull shards and spore slurry. Shooting it too late can still spread the cloud across the fighter's path.
- **Seeds surfaces, not portals.** Anything the burst touches can become contaminated host matter: shield panels, fuel lines, station skin, cargo hulls, or falling debris. That is physical infection consistent with [[CANON]], not a mystical rift.
- **Falls are emergencies.** A damaged casket that survives re-entry can seed a small impact nest or feed an existing breach. [[Starblight]] pilots treat falling caskets as priority targets because [[Deadlane]] crews will pay for every miss.
- **Burn clean or push away.** Flak, fire, shield shove, chaff, and distance all work. The clean kill is a high burn that turns the payload to ash before the cloud touches a carrier surface.

## Gameplay Read

- A **medium orbital hazard** for [[Starblight]]: slower than a [[Scourge-Fighter]], smaller than a boss, dangerous because its death space matters.
- Telegraph: a cold tumbling silhouette, then a toxic-green pulse through cracked hull ribs, then petal plates opening before the burst.
- Counter-play is timing and vector control: detonate it at range, shove it into other Scourge, burn the cloud before it crosses the player, or prioritize falling caskets before they leave the screen toward the ground war.
- Differentiation: unlike [[Descant]], it is not a repeater. Unlike [[Orbital-Breach-Carrier]], it is not a boss carrier. Unlike [[Spore-Lung]], it does not anchor and breathe a room full of fog. It is a one-use coffin of host matter in the sky.

## Visual Motifs

- A [[Scourge-Host-Families|Voidship Host]] made from an escape pod or missile casing: coffin-long, tumbling, armored on one side, split open by black chitin and wet mycelial seams.
- Rusted hull plates wrapped around frozen corpse shapes, bone-white ribs, and spore sacs visible through cracks.
- Petal-shard opening state: outer plates peel back like a broken flower made of ship skin, with tendons holding the payload together until the burst.
- Toxic-green glow only in the seed core and spore slurry, not as decorative ship light.
- Debris-field variants should show old warning paint, severed cable tails, frost, ash scorch, and parasite roots trying to bite into any metal they brush.
