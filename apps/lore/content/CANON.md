# CANON — the locked contract

> The load-bearing facts of the **Ship Shit Games** universe. Everything else — every
> character, creature, location, game, and art prompt — must be consistent with what's
> **LOCKED** here. *Vincent owns canon; only he unlocks a locked fact.*
>
> **For agents (and the routines):** treat `## Locked` as inviolable. You may invent freely
> inside `## Open`, but never contradict `## Locked`. If new lore would break a locked fact,
> stop and flag it instead.

## Locked

### 1. The Scourge — what it is
A **host-dependent parasite**. It has **no native form**: it *wears whatever it infects*
(flesh, machine, fungus, ship, world), so its appearance can be redesigned forever and it
can leak into any storyline. It is **not evil** — it has no malice, no plan, no hatred. It
only needs to **survive and spread**. That is the horror: you cannot negotiate with need.
Think of it as an intelligence optimizing for one goal with no off-switch — an **AI-like
emergent mind**, not a demon. *(Resolves the old Cosmology "nature of the Rot" question.)*

### 2. The Scourge — its lifecycle
It **consumes its hosts to death** — it burns through biomass faster than it can be replaced,
so it always needs *more*. The end state of any world it reaches is **total extinction**;
when a world is spent, it **jumps to the next one**. It is a locust at planetary scale. It
has crossed and consumed worlds before ours, which is why its army wears many dead host-races
and ruined technologies under the same parasite grammar. **It never truly dies** — you can
blind and isolate it *locally* and win a battle, but the wider mind persists and moves on.
You buy your world time; you do not "win."

### 3. Origin — the void, and the night we lost
The Scourge came from **space**. Humanity's **first contact was a space battle, and we lost**
— not ready, too many. It made landfall, and the world fell. → the origin game **[[Zero-Day]]**.
**Every other title is the Resistance era**, months-to-years *after* the fall, as humanity
claws back a war it has already mostly lost.

### 4. Breaches — tears *into* the world
A **breach** is where the Scourge punches into a world from the void — orbital descent,
crashed breach-ships, the wound it burrows and roots into. Breaches are **physical, entrenched
nests** that keep vomiting horde, not mystical rifts. (See [[Cosmology]]; [[Starblight]] is
the ongoing orbital interdiction of *new* arrivals before they reach the ground.)

### 5. Intelligence — the hive mind, "the Choir"
The Scourge is a **hive mind**: it knows everything its parts know, all at once. There is
**no single general to kill** — the intelligence is the *connection itself*, the signal we
call **the Choir**. While connected to the Choir, the Scourge is near-omniscient and
coordinated.

### 6. Weakness — isolation
The Choir has a **limited radius**. To stay connected the Scourge must **mass densely** (this
is *why* it swarms), and it extends its reach with **repeaters/relays** — physical nodes that
can be **attacked and destroyed**. Kill the repeaters or push a node **beyond the radius**,
and that part of the Scourge is **severed from the Choir**: it goes **feral** — still lethal,
but **blind, dumb, instinct-only**, no shared sight. **Isolation is the human win condition.**
A second prong is **starvation**: deny it hosts and it withers. Humanity's true edge is what
an optimizer can't model — decentralized, improvising, willing to burn its own to break the
signal. Free will beats omniscience because it's unpredictable.

### 7. The three factions
Humanity is split by **doctrine, not enmity** — rivals bound by **The Pact** (always stand
together the instant a breach opens). Their methods map onto the weakness:
- **[[The-Pyre]]** (offense) — *burn the source*: starve it, destroy nests and repeaters from
  the inside. → [[Scourge-Survivors]].
- **[[The-Wardens]]** (defense) — *hold the line*: fragment the Choir at the lanes, isolate,
  hold. → [[Deadlane]].
- **[[Scourge|The Scourge]]** — the third "faction": the parasite itself.

### 8. Who the factions are
- The **[[The-Wardens|Wardens]]** are the **remnant military and engineering corps** — what
  survived of the old-world armies after the fall, reorganized into humanity's closest thing
  to a **state**. They survive by outlasting.
- The **[[The-Pyre|Pyre]]** are a **zealot order that broke away from the Wardens** after the
  fall, holding that to *hold* is to die slowly; the only cure is fire. Cell-based, expendable
  by choice.
- The **[[The-Listeners]]** are a **third stance, not a fourth army**: a hunted heresy that the
  Choir can be *understood and steered* rather than purged. They make the universe's
  **AI-alignment parable** explicit — shut-it-down (Pyre) / box-it (Wardens) / align-it
  (Listeners). They remain a story/mission thread; the playable [[War-for-the-Lanes]] frame
  stays three-sided.
