# The Brace Ledger

**At a glance:** [[The-Wardens|Warden]] field practice - a wall crew's live hold sheet, reserve count, break threshold, and [[The-Pact|Pact]] gate promise in one object - turns "hold the line" into measured [[CANON|Choir]] isolation instead of stubborn last stands - appears in [[Deadlane]] / [[War-for-the-Lanes]] / [[Scourge-Survivors]].

The Wardens do not ask whether a wall can hold.

They ask what the hold is buying.

A Brace Ledger is the slate, bolted plate, or cracked field tablet fixed to a lane command post before the first tower warms. It names the wall segment, the crew, the towers tied into it, the ammunition and heat reserve, the [[Tuning-Fork|Fork]] bearings, the nearest repeater read, the evacuation door, the Pyre handoff mark, and the exact condition under which the line must stop being a wall and become a cut. If the sheet cannot say what time, silence, or severance the defense is buying, the [[The-Wardens|Wardens]] do not call it a hold. They call it a pile of bodies waiting to be used.

That is the part the [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] forget when they call the Wardens cowards. A Last Manifest spends one life toward a named cut. A Brace Ledger spends a wall toward a named window. Both exist because [[CANON]] is ugly and specific: the [[Scourge]] is host-dependent, the [[CANON|Choir]] is physical connection, and humans only win locally by starving hosts or isolating repeaters. A wall that keeps the swarm dense and coordinated forever is not courage. It is a feeder trough. A wall that fragments the wave long enough to range a [[Chorister]], launch a Nail, open a gate, or get civilians behind a clean door has bought something real.

Every Ledger has a break line written before the first breach horn. The number changes by lane: tower reserve, wall temperature, ammunition count, casualty load, Fork confidence, Beacon clock, Pyre extraction window. The rule does not. When the wall stops buying isolation and starts offering the Scourge fresh host-mass, the crew either pulls back clean, drops the span, or burns the ground under it. Wardens hate retreat. They hate feeding the Choir more.

Under [[The-Pact]], the Ledger is also a promise. If a Pyre cell is running a [[The-Last-Manifest|Last Manifest]] through the breach, the Warden side of the wall writes the door, the clock, and the last acceptable opening. If the Purger comes back breathing inside that window, the gate opens even if the lane officer hates the cell's creed. If the window closes and the Forks ring green across the carrier path, the same officer marks the door cold and collapses the route before the Choir can wear it back into the city.

The Ledger is not a prayer. It is not mercy. It is the Warden answer to extinction math: count what the line costs, count what the line buys, and stop before pride becomes biomass.

## Ledger Rules

- **Name the purchase.** Every Brace Ledger must state the concrete thing the hold is buying: repeater range, evacuation minutes, Pyre launch/extraction, tower rebuild time, dead-air closeout, or a clean fallback door. No abstract bravery.
- **Map the Choir.** Fork bearings, repeater estimates, pylon reads, and dead-air edges are logged against [[CANON]] sections 5-6. The Ledger cannot promise victory; it can only name how this wall fragments the local signal.
- **Count the reserve.** Towers, Nails, Beacon charges, heat draws, ammunition, wall anchors, med space, and crew rotations are counted before the wave hits. A Warden line can spend itself, but it must know what it is spending.
- **Write the break line early.** The collapse, retreat, burn, or gate-seal threshold is set while the crew is still calm. No officer gets to invent heroics after the wall is already full of dead.
- **Honor the Pact door.** During a live breach response, Pyre handoff marks and extraction clocks are part of the Ledger. The Wardens do not adopt Pyre doctrine; they keep the promised door honest until the Choir makes it unsafe.
- **Close the carriers.** After the hold, the Ledger is not complete until the crew runs [[The-Dead-Air-Liturgy]]: wires counted, corpses cleared, blood sealed, warm machines isolated, and any route that could carry the Choir marked dead or rebuilt clean.

## Gameplay Read

- A **mission contract and pressure meter** for [[Deadlane]]: what the wall is defending, which repeater must be ranged, when to spend tower reserve, and when the correct play is to drop a span instead of holding it one wave too long.
- A **Warline operation receipt**: a successful defense shifts the front because the Ledger bought something legible - five evacuation minutes, one severed lane pocket, one Pyre extraction, one clean fallback - not because a wall magically solved the war.
- A **Pact tension hook** for [[Scourge-Survivors]] and [[Pactfall]]: grudges can start over a gate opened late, a Pyre cell marked unverified, a Warden officer accused of sealing too soon, or a heat draw that left another wall thin.
- Failure is visible and procedural: a crossed-out Fork bearing, a break line ignored, a carrier path left green, a wall held after it became host supply, or a gate opened into a connected route.

## Visual / Prop Brief

- A battered Warden wall slate, not a clean command tablet: gunmetal frame, bone-white ceramic inserts, grease-pencil grids, riveted tower tags, red break-line marks, and ash-stained clock notches.
- The silhouette is practical and bolted down. It hangs beside a tower controller, gate winch, or firing step where a crew can slap new counts onto it under smoke.
- Colors stay in [[Style-Bible]] discipline: gunmetal, bone, ash, blood-red strike marks, rust, and hellfire heat stamps. Toxic-green appears only as Scourge signal bleed on a failed carrier or active Fork mark.
- Used variants should show the truth of the wall: cracked corners, thumb-worn tower tags, dried blood wiped across a reserve count, burn marks around a deleted route, and one clean line where the break threshold was set before panic.
