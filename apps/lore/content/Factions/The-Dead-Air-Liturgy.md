# The Dead-Air Liturgy

**At a glance:** [[The-Pact|Pact]] field drill - the call-and-response checklist crews run after a [[Tuning-Fork]] finds the Choir, a [[Blackout-Nail]] cuts it, and a [[Dead-Air-Beacon]] holds the wound - keeps humans from gifting the [[CANON|Choir]] a fresh physical carrier - not a prayer, jammer, or Listener rite - appears in [[Deadlane]] / [[Redline]] / [[Scourge-Survivors]].

The Dead-Air Liturgy is the only prayer the [[The-Wardens|Wardens]] allow on the wall.

They call it a checklist when officers are listening. The [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] call it a liturgy because the words are said under ash, blood, and a clock nobody expects to beat. Both names survive because both are useful. A scared crew remembers a chant better than a manual. A smart crew remembers the chant is for humans, not for the [[Scourge]].

The Liturgy begins after the cut. A Fork has found the loud side. A Nail has burned the carrier. A Beacon is hissing in the fresh wound. For a few minutes, the local path is dead air: no easy root, no clean cable, no wet body bridge, no dense host-mass for the Choir to ride back through. That silence is not safety. It is work.

So the crew speaks the work out loud.

Count the wire. Count the water. Count the corpse. Count the warm gun. Count the open door. Count the blood on the floor. Every line answers a field mistake that once killed people: a power lead dragged across a Beacon ring, a dead runner left half in the ash, a breathing casualty carried through a cable throat, a Warden pylon reconnected too early, a Pyre tank venting heat into a root seam. The words do not weaken the Choir. The words keep hands from making the path whole again.

That distinction matters because [[CANON]] is clear: the Choir is the connection, not a demon listening for holy syllables. The Scourge is host-dependent and survival-driven. It will use any body, root, wire, hull, stone, or machine it can wear. The Liturgy is the Pact's ugly answer to that fact: if the enemy needs physical continuity, then every human in the dead-air pocket becomes responsible for not building continuity by accident.

At [[The-Switchback]], the Liturgy is painted on severance plates and shouted over winches while a deck drops. At [[Holdout-Nine]], it is scratched into route slates before a courier leaves the basement. In [[Perdition]], a Pyre operator mutters the lines alone because the extraction corridor has started to sing again and nobody on the far side can hear them yet.

The last line is always the same.

Leave nothing that can carry the song.

## Field Rules

- **Cut first.** The Liturgy never creates dead air by itself. It starts only after a verified [[Tuning-Fork]] range, physical severance, distance break, or [[Dead-Air-Beacon]] placement consistent with [[CANON]] sections 5-6.
- **Count every carrier.** Crews mark and clear anything the Scourge could wear across the pocket: roots, cables, rail, water lines, corpse piles, blood trails, hot engines, exposed bone, and open breach tissue.
- **Keep command physical.** Inside the pocket, orders move by hand sign, slate, colored ash tags, rope pulls, or runners. The rule is not that radio is magic-bad; the rule is that no powered line, wire path, or repeater mast gets reconnected until the carrier count is clean.
- **Move the living fast.** Wounded humans are host-mass and heat. The drill prioritizes sealing blood, moving casualties off the ash edge, and burning contaminated gear before a feral pocket finds a bridge back to the Choir.
- **Close before pride.** Wardens may want the lane rebuilt. Pyre may want the whole route burned. Under [[The-Pact]], neither gets to skip the closeout: Beacon clock, carrier count, extraction mark, and fallback door.
- **Abort on green.** Toxic-green pulse crossing the ash ring means the pocket is no longer clean. The Liturgy stops being a checklist and becomes a retreat, burn, or collapse order.

## Gameplay Read

- A **diegetic objective checklist** for isolation encounters: find the signal, cut the carrier, hold the Beacon, then clear the physical paths that would let the Choir reconnect.
- In [[Deadlane]], it becomes a Warden lane phase: crews must clear cables, corpses, and pylon stubs before rebuilding a tower line through a dead-air choke.
- In [[Redline]], it explains route marks and no-transmit courier discipline through [[The-Hollow-Lanes]]: a runner carries the message because the lane cannot be made into a speaking wire.
- In [[Scourge-Survivors]], it gives extraction corridors readable failure beats: blood trail left across a ring, a hot door motor lighting green, a body bridge that must be burned before the room gets smart again.
- In [[War-for-the-Lanes]], it is a compact operation result: a local cut held because the crew closed the carriers, not because the wider Scourge was defeated.

## Visual / Prop Brief

- A brutal field card, wall stencil, or ash-tag strip rather than scripture: bone-white ceramic, oil-black slate, red severance marks, Warden hazard ticks, Pyre scorch knots.
- Marks are big enough to read through smoke: wire, root, body, blood, heat, door, clock. No elegant script, no illuminated manuscript, no clean sci-fi interface.
- The human palette stays [[Style-Bible]]-disciplined: gunmetal, bone, ash, blood, rust, and hellfire cautery. Toxic-green appears only as incoming Scourge signal bleed at a failed carrier.
- Set dressing should show use: thumb-worn cards, cracked tags tied to Beacon legs, checklist plates riveted beside dropped spans, and route slates with fresh blood wiped off before the next line is written.

## Appears In

- [[Deadlane]] - Warden crews recite the carrier count while holding a severed lane and deciding which infrastructure can be rebuilt without giving the Choir a road.
- [[Redline]] - couriers learn the Liturgy as route discipline: do not reconnect the lane behind you, do not drag a carrier across the ash, do not make the message louder than the runner.
- [[Scourge-Survivors]] - Pyre Purgers use the drill during extraction when a [[Dead-Air-Beacon]] has bought minutes and the breach is trying to stitch the corridor closed.
- [[The-Hush]] - treated as civic instinct more than doctrine; the holdout lives by the same principle, only stretched across a city.
