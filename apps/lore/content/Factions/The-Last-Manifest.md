# The Last Manifest

**At a glance:** [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] field practice - a Purger's final run sheet, oath, fuel ledger, and [[The-Pact|Pact]] handoff in one document - turns "expendable by choice" into a targetable cut against the [[CANON|Choir]], not blind martyrdom - appears in [[Scourge-Survivors]] / [[Pactfall]] / [[War-for-the-Lanes]].

The Pyre do not ask if you are willing to die.

They ask what the death is supposed to buy.

A Last Manifest is the paper, slate, or burnt ceramic tag that answers before a Purger leaves [[The-Pyre-Gate]]. It names the operator, the cell, the kit, the charged tanks drawn from [[The-Cinder-Wells]], the [[Tuning-Fork|Fork]] bearing, the [[Blackout-Nail|Nail]] count, the expected extraction window, and the thing the run is meant to cut: a repeater, a carrier path, a host nest, a breach organ, a door that lets the next team go deeper. If the sheet cannot name what the life is buying, the muster master sends the operator back to the rack.

That is the part outsiders miss. The Pyre are fanatics, but they are not careless. [[CANON]] is clear about the war: the [[Scourge]] is host-dependent, the Choir lives in physical connection, and humans only win locally by starving hosts or isolating nodes. A Purger who dies in a hallway for no cut, no burn, no severance, and no carried signal is not holy. They are wasted. The Manifest exists so the order can tell the difference.

The first versions were old Warden casualty forms after [[Timeline|the Schism]], stripped of rank columns and rewritten in ash. The [[The-Wardens|Wardens]] had used them to count what a defense cost. The Pyre used them to count what an attack must accomplish before the cost was acceptable. Under [[The-Pact]], a Warden lane officer now countersigns the field copy when a breach is live: not because the Wardens approve of the creed, but because somebody has to promise the door will open if the runner comes back breathing.

Most do not. The Last Manifest gets filed anyway.

At the Gate, the living copy is tied to the Purger's harness in a bone-white sleeve. The wall copy stays with the launch crew. The dead copy is a narrow ceramic strip pushed into the burn ledger after the run: black if the target failed, blood-red if the cut held long enough for the line to move, hellfire-marked if the operator returned and signed the margin themselves. That last mark is rare enough that nobody jokes when it happens.

The Pyre call it witness. Wardens call it accounting. Both are right. The paper does not make the death clean. It keeps the death aimed.

## Manifest Rules

- **Name the cut.** Every Manifest must identify the physical thing the run is meant to break, burn, or hold open: repeater, carrier, host cache, breach organ, extraction door, or dead-air pocket. No abstract glory. No "kill as many as possible."
- **State the Choir logic.** The target must map to [[CANON]] §5-6: sever the local Choir path, starve host supply, hold isolation with a [[Dead-Air-Beacon]], or open the way to a deeper node. The Manifest cannot promise a permanent win.
- **Count the heat.** Tanks, cells, Nails, Beacon charges, and tower reserve drawn from [[The-Cinder-Wells]] are listed before launch. A Pyre cell can spend itself freely; it cannot pretend the wall paid nothing.
- **Bind the handoff.** During a live breach response, a Warden or Pact runner records the lane clock, extraction signal, and fallback door. The Pact coordinates the run without softening either doctrine.
- **Close the sheet.** A Manifest ends in one of four marks: cut held, cut failed, operator recovered, or operator unverified. Unverified is the mark everyone hates, because it means the Choir may have learned from the body.

## Gameplay Read

- A **mission contract and run summary object** for [[Scourge-Survivors]]: what node the run is chasing, what gear was spent, whether the cut actually bought isolation, and what the extraction clock meant.
- A **political pressure hook** for [[Pactfall]]: arena grudges can start over a countersign, a disputed heat draw, or a Warden claim that a Pyre cell spent line resources on a bad cut.
- A **Warline operation receipt**: the player can see why a successful purge shifted pressure in one region instead of magically winning the war. The local lane got time because one Manifest named the right thing and the run paid for it.
- Failure is legible: a crossed-out Fork bearing, a cold Nail count, a Beacon charge returned unused, or an operator marked unverified after entering a connected carrier path.

## Visual / Prop Brief

- A scorched ceramic or oilskin run sheet, not parchment fantasy: clipped to a Pyre harness, stamped by a Warden lane clock, and heat-blistered at the edges.
- Colors stay in [[Style-Bible]] discipline: bone-white ceramic, blood-red failed/held marks, hellfire wax or cautery seals, gunmetal clips, ash grime. No neon, no clean sci-fi blue.
- The readable silhouette is the tag sleeve on the chest rig or belt: small, flat, rectangular, deliberately easy to tear free before a body is burned.
- Late-run variants should show sweat, blood, soot, and toxic-green contamination only as Scourge signal residue on the edge - never as a Pyre decorative color.
