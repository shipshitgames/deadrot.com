# Redline

**At a glance:** high-speed courier platformer (Sonic-like) · outrun the Choir — hold the redline or the swarm catches up (speed beats omniscience) · lone Pyre / Warden runner · the courier front of the War for the Lanes, turning the [[CANON]] §6 win condition into a verb (Resistance-era).

*Outrun the Choir.* When [[Scourge|the Scourge]] cuts the [[Cosmology|lanes]] and chokes
the comms between holdouts, the message still has to get through — so a lone runner *carries
it on foot*, faster than the horde can close. **Speed is survival.** Stop moving and the
swarm catches up; the Choir is always listening for the one thing still moving on the dead
roads.

## Canon role
The courier front of the war. [[Deadlane]] holds the lanes, [[Scourge-Survivors]] descends
into the breaches, [[Starblight]] burns the orbit — **Redline keeps the holdouts talking**
when the breaches sever the wired relays and the Scourge floods the corridors between them.
A severed holdout is a dead holdout; the runner is the human repeater the Choir can't jam.
Both factions field couriers under [[Timeline|the Pact]] — a Pyre runner carries a burn
order, a Warden runner carries a hold order, and on the road they run the same gauntlet.

## The hook — speed beats omniscience
The Scourge is near-omniscient *while it's massed and connected* ([[CANON]] §5), but its
coordination has a tempo, and a runner at full **redline** is faster than the Choir can
re-route the horde to cut them off. The fantasy is the [[CANON]] §6 win condition turned into
a verb: you don't fight the signal, you **outpace** it. Lose your momentum and the swarm's
omniscience snaps shut around you.

## Core Loop
Flow-state, readable high-speed platforming layered on the shared [[Survivors-Loop]]:
launch a run → chain ramps, rail-grinds, and breach-debris boost pads to hold the redline →
bank salvage/XP at speed → draft movement/loadout upgrades → reach the next holdout before the
gate closes (or get caught and overrun). Levels are **lanes-as-racecourses**: collapsing
chokepoints, breach-flooded streets, and Choir-repeater jammers you blow past or smash through.

## Enemy shape
The Scourge here is the road itself turning against you: chitin growing over the asphalt,
spore-walls that bloom across the lane, [[Render]]-class horrors that try to cut you off at
junctions, and **repeater-pylons** whose jamming radius slows the redline — clip them and the
local horde goes feral and stops predicting you.
