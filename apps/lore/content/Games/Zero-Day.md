# Zero Day

**At a glance:** [[Starblight]] prologue / flashback mode · first-contact last stand in orbit and atmosphere · *the night we lost the sky* — you do not win, you buy minutes and witness the fall · pre-schism humanity · the origin / anchor event of the [[Timeline]] and the loss that breaks humanity into [[The-Pyre]] vs [[The-Wardens]] ([[CANON]] §3).

*The night we lost the sky.* Zero Day is no longer a separate public game in the roster. It is
the first battle [[Starblight]] carries inside it: a black-box memory of humanity's **first
contact** with [[Scourge|the Scourge]], fought in orbit and atmosphere before anyone knows what
a breach costs. **You do not win.** You keep the guns hot, clear a few evacuation lanes, watch
the orbital defense fold, and then the world learns the shape of after.

## Canon role
The anchor event of the [[Timeline]] and the [[Premise]]: the Scourge arrives from the void,
humanity is **not ready and too many**, the orbital and sky defense collapses, and the
breaches root groundside. After Zero Day there is no "before" — every other title is the
**Resistance era**. (See [[CANON]] §3.) [[Starblight]] is what humanity builds from the wreckage:
the Skyhook, the interceptor corps, and the nightly work of keeping the same sky from falling
again.

## Starblight relationship

Product-wise, Zero Day is a **mode / prologue / flashback sequence inside [[Starblight]]**, not
a competing arcade space shooter. The clean sentence is:

> Zero Day is when the sky fell. Starblight is what remains of humanity trying to keep it from
> falling again every night.

Use Zero Day when the run should feel historical, doomed, and pre-schism: no Pyre, no Wardens,
no clean doctrine yet. Use Starblight for the playable Resistance-era front: named pilots,
Skyhook infrastructure, Pyre/Warden loadouts, and orbital interdiction of new arrivals.

## The hook: the unwinnable battle
The drama is the loss. Waves escalate past any sustainable defense; the player buys minutes,
saves *some* evacuations, witnesses the fall. The fantasy is heroism inside doom — the seed
of the whole studio's tone: brutal, fast, gallows-humor, *we lost the sky but we're still
shooting*.

## Hooks
- Shared horde / rules / look with every title: [[Scourge]], [[Cosmology]], [[Style-Bible]].
- Sets up the **Choir** and its **repeaters** (see [[CANON]] §5–6): first appearance of the
  orbital relays that [[Starblight]] later hunts through the Skyhook.
- Pre-schism: this is *before* [[The-Pyre]] and [[The-Wardens]] split — the loss is what
  breaks humanity into "burn the source" vs "hold the line."
