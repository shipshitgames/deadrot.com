# Ashgate

**At a glance:** [[The-Wardens|Warden]] capital · where three lanes converge · foundry output (ammunition, breach-shielding, [[CANON|repeaters]]) · primary [[Deadlane]] theater · [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] forward camp + Purger launch · [[Pactfall]] arena district · holds because retreat = extinction.

Ask a Warden where the war is and they won't name a front. They'll say *the wall* — and there's only one wall they mean.

Ashgate made things, before [[Zero-Day]]. Foundries, fabrication yards, transit lines, and not one defensive structure worth the name. After the fall that turned out to be the thing that saved it: while cities built for living went dark, Ashgate kept building — ammunition, breach-shielding, and the [[CANON|repeater]] mounts that keep the [[Scourge|Scourge's]] signal cut to pieces at the city's edge. The [[The-Wardens|Wardens]] made it their capital, not for its walls — it had none — but for its output, and for the fact that three lanes meet here. Hold Ashgate, hold the network. Lose it, and the Resistance falls apart into pockets the [[CANON|Choir]] can swallow one at a time.

On the war-map this foundry district is marked the **Foundry Wards**, and the [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] camp that shares the eastern approach sits just outside it at the adjacent [[The-Pyre-Gate]].

So the Wardens built the wall they should have started with. The eastern approach — the widest breach lane, the one the swarm comes down hardest — is sealed now under a rampart of towers, kill-corridors, and artillery, stacked out of a decade of holding and not dying. The outer towers fall and get retaken in the same shift. By morning the dead Scourge is packed against the outer berm six deep, and the clearing crews go out to cut it back before it starts to grow.

The name is the Pyre's fault. The [[The-Pyre]] keep a camp at the eastern gate and sling their Purgers from Ashgate's forward towers into whatever just punched through; the ash off their burns never quite settles. The Wardens hate the smell and can't run the war without the people making it. The two factions share the wall and nothing else — bunked side by side, drawing the same supply lines, always one bad shift from each other's throats. The only place they're allowed to settle it is the arena district, where sanctioned [[Pactfall]] bouts bleed off what the [[Premise|Pact]] won't let them spend in the field.

Nobody in Ashgate talks about winning. They talk about the next shift. The foundries run, the wall holds, the dead pile up and get cleared, and the city keeps standing for the one reason that has never once failed it: there is nowhere behind it to go.

## Role in the war
A **holdout** — the [[The-Wardens|Warden]] capital and the primary [[Deadlane]] theater, the one place three lanes converge. Its value was never walls (it had none) but **output and position**: the foundries that arm the Resistance and the [[CANON|repeater]] crown that keeps the [[CANON|Choir]] signal fragmented at the city's edge. Hold Ashgate and the lane network stays coherent; lose it and the Resistance splinters into pockets the Choir can swallow one at a time (see [[Cosmology]]; [[CANON]] §6 — isolation is the win condition). It does not break the signal so much as keep the lanes behind it survivable for the Wardens who do.
