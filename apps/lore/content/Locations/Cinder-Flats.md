# Cinder Flats

**At a glance:** a burned plain of crashed breach-ships · holds [[CANON|The Cinder Breach]] · where the beached hulk [[The-Rothulk]] lies grown into the ground · the approach a [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] saboteur crosses to reach a breach-ship's core.

The Scourge doesn't always burrow in. Sometimes it falls.

The Flats are where the breach-ships came down — a dead plain east of the lanes, flat to the horizon and the color of ash because that's mostly what it is. Every craft that crashed here burned on the way through the sky and went on burning where it landed, and the things inside crawled out into the open and started growing. The ground is scorched glass and clinker, studded with hulls the size of hills, each one a breach in its own right, each one rooting tendrils down into the burned earth and pulling up whatever it can reach. The Pyre charts the whole field as one wound — [[CANON|The Cinder Breach]] — because the ships are close enough that the [[CANON|Choir]] runs between them, hull to hull, across the dead flat with nothing to break the signal.

Nothing grows here that didn't fall. There's no rock to corrupt, no lane-floor to burrow under, just the hulks and the char between them and the long sightlines that get a saboteur killed if a single repeater is still lit. A Pyre runner crossing the Flats keeps low and keeps to the burn-scars, because the open ground reads you to every ship at once.

The biggest of the wrecks has a name. It came down hardest, sank deepest, and grew into the earth instead of just sitting on it — a mountain of rotting host-flesh and dead-world machinery that the Pyre stopped calling a ship a long time ago. They call it [[The-Rothulk]], and crossing the Flats is mostly the business of getting to its base alive so someone can climb it and burn it from the inside.

## Role in the war
**Breach** — a crash-field of breach-ships, the [[CANON|crashed-breach-ship]] case from [[CANON]] §4 written across a whole plain rather than a single wreck. It holds [[CANON|The Cinder Breach]]: many hulls, one connected nest, the [[CANON|Choir]] running hull to hull across open ground. In the war it is an approach and a staging field — the burned ground a [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] saboteur crosses to reach [[The-Rothulk]] and ignite its core, severing the local node ([[Cosmology|The Purge]]).
