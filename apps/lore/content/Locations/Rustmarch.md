# Rustmarch

**At a glance:** a rusting industrial march on the north front · the ground between [[The-Spire]] and the breaches · contested, held by no one for long · changes hands every few shifts; the front line is wherever you happen to be standing.

The old maps call it a manufacturing district. The Wardens call it the march, and they say it the way you'd say a stretch of bad weather — something you cross, not somewhere you live.

Rustmarch is iron all the way down. Foundry stacks, ore lines, slag fields, a horizon of dead machines too heavy to move and too big to clear, all of it gone the deep oxblood color that gives the place its name. The wind here carries grit, and the grit is rust, and after a shift on the march it's under your nails and in your teeth. The Scourge loves it. Machine-graft hosts come up out of the wreck-fields wearing the same iron the Wardens are walking on, and at a hundred meters in failing light you can't always tell which heaps are dead and which are about to stand.

This is the north front: the long, exposed ground between [[The-Spire|the Spire]] at one end and the breaches at the other, and nobody holds it for long. The Wardens push a line of anchors and gun platforms forward, the breaches vomit until the line buckles, the line falls back, the Pyre burns what's left, and in three shifts it all happens again the other direction. There is no wall on the march. There's nowhere to put one that the rust won't eat.

A Wallwright who'd done four rotations on the march put it plainest, scraping a fresh weld that was already going orange: *you don't hold Rustmarch. You rent it, and the rent is everybody you brought.*

## Role in the war
A **Lane** front in the [[Cosmology|Cosmology]] sense — contested ground between a holdout and the breaches — but an open, attritional one rather than a fortified chokepoint. Unlike [[Deadlane|Deadlane]]'s held wall, the march has no line that stays put: it is fought across, traded shift by shift, and feeds the [[The-Spire|Spire]] the same way the eastern lanes feed [[Ashgate]]. Its value is the iron and the foundry stacks; whoever works them last, works them only until the next push.
