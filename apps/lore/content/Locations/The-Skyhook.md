# The Skyhook

**At a glance:** the orbital ring and tether above the surface war · humanity's last interdiction line, burning [[Scourge]] breach-matter out of the sky before it falls into the lanes · contested, held by no one cleanly · the theater of [[Starblight]] · the orbital interdiction of new arrivals first lost on [[Zero-Day]].

Look up from the lanes on a clear night and you can see it: a thin bright scratch across the dark, and every so often a streak of fire coming down off it. The streak is the Skyhook doing its job. The thing that streak was — that's the job.

The Skyhook is what humanity still holds of orbit. A battered ring and the tether that anchors it, salvaged and re-crewed after [[Zero-Day]] took the sky and never gave it back. The Scourge doesn't stop arriving when a world starts to fall; it keeps coming down the same dark it came down the first night — spores, infected wreckage, living carrier-ships, raw breach-matter drifting in to seed fresh nests on the ground. The Skyhook's whole purpose is to catch it up here, where it's thin and scattered, instead of down there, where every chunk that lands becomes a breach in a street somebody's defending.

So the pilots burn it. Every interception is breach-matter that never roots in the lanes — every carrier broken in orbit is a breach that never opens at Ashgate's wall.

A Pyre interceptor pilot, asked why she burned a contaminated drifter that was already falling away from the ring and would have missed everything, didn't look up from the rearm. *It misses us,* she said. *It doesn't miss them.* She meant the lanes. That's the math the whole ring runs on: a thing that gets past the Skyhook is a thing the holdouts have to kill twice as hard, on the ground, with people who can't fly away.

## Role in the war
A **Lane** in the truest sense — the highest one, the [[Cosmology|Cosmology]] corridor the Scourge itself uses to *arrive*. The Skyhook is where humanity contests the breach **before it becomes a breach**, the orbital interdiction of new arrivals first attempted (and lost) on [[Zero-Day]] and held, barely, ever since. It does not isolate the Choir the way the ground lanes do; it starves the ground of fresh hosts and fresh nests, the [[CANON]] §6 starvation prong fought from above. Hold the Skyhook and the surface war stays the war it is. Lose it and the sky becomes a breach again.
