# The Spire

**At a glance:** [[The-Wardens|Warden]] command bastion + long-range artillery hold · north of [[Ashgate]] on the Spire Causeway · sees the lanes before [[Ashgate]] does · the only hold that has never fallen · directs fire for [[Deadlane]].

North of [[Ashgate]], where the causeway climbs above the lanes, the Wardens kept the one piece of high ground that mattered and turned it into a gun. The Spire is a watch-hold first: from its crown a [[The-Wardens|Warden]] spotter reads the eastern approach a full shift before the wall does, and counts the swarm massing while it's still a smudge on the horizon. Then it's a battery. The long guns up there don't aim — they're handed coordinates from the spotters and the lane controllers below, and they drop fire on a kill-corridor a courier-ride away while the gunners never once see what they hit.

Ask the crews and they'll tell you the worst nights aren't the loud ones. It's the silence after the spotters go quiet, when the only sound on the causeway is the breech cycling and someone reading numbers off a slate. Down at the wall they curse the Spire when the shells walk short. They pray to it when the towers fold and the only thing turning the swarm is fire from above that nobody asked for and everybody needs.

It has never fallen. The Wardens say it the way you'd touch wood — not a boast, a load-bearing fact. Lose the Spire and Ashgate goes blind, then the guns turn the wrong way, then there's no wall. So the causeway stays held, the spotters keep counting, and the long guns keep their throats warm against the cold that comes off the lanes.

## Role in the war
A **holdout** — the rearward command-and-fire node of the Ashgate network, not a front line of its own. It buys the wall foresight and reach: early warning down the lanes and artillery that fragments the swarm before the [[CANON|Choir]] can mass it densely enough to coordinate (see [[Cosmology]]; [[CANON]] §6). It does not break the signal itself; it makes the lanes below survivable long enough for the Wardens who do.
