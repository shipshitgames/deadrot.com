# Blackout Nail

**At a glance:** Pact-shared severance charge · a sacrificial spike driven into a repeater,
root, or stolen line to physically cut the **Choir** · creates the short blackout window
humans need after a [[Tuning-Fork]] finds the node ([[CANON]] §6) · appears in [[Deadlane]] /
[[Scourge-Survivors]] / [[Redline]].

The [[Tuning-Fork]] tells you where the song is loudest. The **Blackout Nail** is what you
drive into the throat when you get there.

It is not a jammer, not a clean signal weapon, and not a way to win the war from behind a
wall. The Choir is carried through bodies, roots, cables, hull, stone, and stolen organs
([[CANON]] §5-6), so the Nail attacks the **carrier**, not the idea. A Warden crew fires one
into a ringing pylon. A Pyre Purger hammers one through a wet resonator sac in the dark. A
courier clips one into a [[Carillon]] line without slowing down. The charge blooms inward,
burning the physical path the relay is using to stay connected, and for a few seconds the
world around it goes blessedly, murderously quiet.

That silence is the only product. The Nail does not kill the Scourge. It does not teach the
Choir fear. It forces a local severance: the hosts riding that repeater drop feral, blind,
dumb, instinct-only, until another relay grows around the wound or the swarm masses densely
enough to carry the signal again ([[CANON]] §6). If the Nail lands shallow, the Choir routes
around it. If it lands true, every human in the window moves.

## How it works

- **Physical severance, not magic.** The Nail is a dense gunmetal spike wrapped around a
  host-bone conductor, a collapsible grounding braid, and a one-use cauterizer core. It
  needs a repeater organ, root bundle, tower graft, cable line, or breach tendon to bite.
- **Fork first, Nail second.** A [[Tuning-Fork]] ranges the loudest source and the radius edge;
  the Nail only matters once the crew has found the carrier. It is the execution tool, not the
  detector.
- **One window, no permanence.** On a true hit, the local relay path burns open and nearby
  connected Scourge drop feral. The Choir persists beyond the cut ([[CANON]] §2), and the
  Scourge will spend bodies to re-root a [[Chorister]], loft a [[Quaver]], or rush a
  [[Cantor]] into the gap.
- **Doctrine changes the casing.** Wardens use heavy wall-fired Nails, braced into lane guns
  and pylon charges. Pyre cells carry shorter hand Nails, ugly enough to be hammered into a
  living breach organ with a rifle butt. Same principle, different willingness to stand close.
- **Misuse is lethal.** A Nail driven into ordinary host flesh only enrages the body. A Nail
  driven into a connected carrier buys silence. The difference is why Fork operators are
  treated like ammunition and medics in the same breath.

## The cost

Every Nail has to be placed **inside the Choir's working radius**, where the swarm is smartest.
That is the design flaw and the design purpose. The charge is too short-lived to use as a
shield and too crude to fire blindly. Someone has to hear the repeater, reach the carrier, and
keep their hands steady while everything in range already knows they are coming.

Wardens hate spending Nails because the conductors are scarce and the window is brief. Pyre
Purgers love them for the same reason: brief is enough if you are already running toward the
source. The [[The-Listeners|Listeners]] steal them intact when they can, not to silence the
Choir forever, but to make one node stop screaming long enough to be taken alive.

## Gameplay Read

- A **severance consumable / objective tool**, not a general weapon. It turns a found repeater
  into a timed blackout window: nearby Scourge lose coordination, lanes loosen, and the player
  gets the push or escape chance the encounter was built around.
- Requires **target ID and access**. The player still has to locate the carrier with a Fork,
  expose the right organ or line, and land the Nail under pressure.
- Strongest against relay infrastructure: [[Chorister]] roots, [[Bourdon]] throat-wall veins,
  [[Carillon]] cables, and lane repeater pylons. Weak against ordinary swarm bodies.
- Telegraphs severance diegetically: the toxic-green pulse snaps backward into the Nail, the
  grounding braid glows blood-hot, then the sound cuts out and the horde's formation slackens.
- The blackout is a window, not a solved state. Another relay can close the gap if the player
  hesitates.

## Sprite / Item Brief

- A brutal sacrificial spike: squat gunmetal wedge, riveted casing, cracked bone-conductor
  core exposed along one edge, wrapped in a black grounding braid that unspools on impact.
- Warden variant: long, heavy, launcher-fed, with bracing fins and hazard stencils worn to ash.
  Reads as wall hardware and demolition gear.
- Pyre variant: shorter hand Nail with scorch tape, prayer-scratched casing, and a pull-ring
  cauterizer cap. Reads as something carried into a breach and not expected to come back.
- Toxic-green is captured signal bleed only, flaring at the point when it bites; the human
  device itself stays blood, rust, gunmetal, bone, and hellfire per [[Style-Bible]].

## Appears in

- [[Deadlane]] — Warden crews fire wall-Nails into [[Chorister]] roots, [[Cantor]] lanes, or a
  [[Carillon]] pylon to black out a wave long enough to rebuild, rotate guns, or extract a
  crew from a collapsing lane.
- [[Scourge-Survivors]] — a Pyre Purger carries two or three hand Nails into [[Perdition]];
  one true hit on a [[Bourdon]] or breach-room repeater can make the room go feral just long
  enough to push deeper.
- [[Redline]] — couriers clip Nails into ringing grid grafts at speed, severing the
  [[Carillon]] line so the next junction stops predicting the run.
