# Dead-Air Beacon

**At a glance:** Pact-shared isolation field kit · a temporary dead-air marker that keeps a
severed Choir path from reconnecting by burning carrier material out of the ground · placed
after a [[Tuning-Fork]] ranges the edge and a [[Blackout-Nail]] or hard cut severs the node
([[CANON]] §6) · appears in [[Deadlane]] / [[Redline]] / [[Scourge-Survivors]].

A Dead-Air Beacon is not a jammer. The [[CANON|Choir]] is not a radio you can drown out from
safe ground, and the [[Scourge]] does not lose because humans built a louder box. The Choir
travels through bodies, roots, cables, hull, stone, and anything else the parasite can wear.
The Beacon works because it attacks the thing the signal needs most: a physical path.

Wardens call it a beacon because it marks the silence. Pyre cells call it a cold nail because
it does not kill anything worth bragging about. Both are right. A crew plants the tripod at the
edge of a ranged node, drives its bone-conductor feet through the ash, and opens the cautery
cells. For a few minutes the ground around it becomes useless to the Choir: tendrils crisp,
wet carrier roots glass over, dead cable stubs fuse shut, and anything small enough to bridge
the gap burns before it can become a relay. The local horde does not die. It loses the easy
way back to the song.

That is all the Beacon buys: **dead air**. If a [[Chorister]] is still singing, the Beacon
will not silence it. If a [[Cantor]] reaches the far side with enough bodies around it, the
Choir routes around the burn. If a [[Carillon]] still owns the overhead wire, the street is
quiet only until the next mast rings. But after a true cut - a Nail through the carrier, a
collapsed pylon, a severed root - a Beacon can hold the wound open long enough for humans to
move. In a war where isolation is the only local win condition ([[CANON]] §6), a few minutes
of silence is the difference between an extraction and a wall full of names.

## How it works

- **Dead-air, not denial of thought.** The Beacon does not confuse the Choir or overwrite the
  Scourge's mind. It clears a small patch of usable carrier material so the signal cannot
  easily re-root through that spot.
- **Fork finds the edge.** A [[Tuning-Fork]] marks where the Choir drops thin; the Beacon is
  placed there, not thrown blindly into a wave. Bad placement makes a hot lamp in a smart
  swarm.
- **Nail cuts, Beacon holds.** A [[Blackout-Nail]] severs the physical carrier. The Beacon
  keeps the burned path from knitting shut while crews cross, rebuild, reload, or run.
- **Host-starvation in miniature.** The Beacon is the [[CANON]] §6 starvation principle
  reduced to field gear: deny hosts, deny roots, deny cable, deny the parasite an easy body to
  wear.
- **It has a clock.** Once the cautery cells cool and ash clogs the feet, the dead-air pocket
  collapses. The Scourge will spend bodies to bridge it if the fight lasts long enough.

## The cost

Every Beacon has to be carried into the radius where the Choir is still smart enough to
understand what the crew is doing. Wardens mount them on wall sleds and pylon carts, bracing
the tripod under tower fire. Pyre Purgers carry smaller coffin-beacons on their backs and drop
them in breach corridors when the extraction route starts to sing. Couriers in [[Redline]]
use road beacons built to unfold at speed, ugly little things that bite asphalt and burn out
behind them.

They are also expensive in the one material no holdout can replace easily: clean ground. A
Beacon leaves a circle where nothing useful grows, roots, or conducts. That is acceptable on
[[The-Hollow-Lanes|the Hollow Lanes]], where the roads are already dying. It is a harder
argument inside a holdout garden, under a water line, or anywhere the [[The-Wardens|Wardens]]
expected to rebuild after the breach closed. The [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] do not care. The Wardens
keep count.

The [[The-Listeners|Listeners]] hate Beacons for a different reason. Dead air is good war and
bad study. A Beacon can save a crew by keeping a severed node quiet, but it also burns away the
timing marks and carrier behavior a [[The-Quiet-Rooms|Quiet Room]] would have used to learn
the route back to the Choir. In the field, the [[The-Pact]] settles the argument: if silence
buys the breach window, the Beacon goes down.

## Gameplay Read

- A **temporary isolation tool / deployable objective**, not a damage item. It creates a small
  dead-air pocket after the player identifies or cuts the repeater path.
- Strongest in encounters built around reconnection pressure: [[Cantor]] reroutes, [[Carillon]]
  lines, breach-room roots, and lane pylons trying to re-light after a Nail hit.
- Telegraphs clearly: toxic-green pulses crawl toward the Beacon, snap into blood-hot cautery
  sparks at the ring edge, then fade to black ash. When the cells cool, the green begins to
  creep back in.
- Creates tactical choices: hold the dead-air ring, move through it, or abandon it before the
  Scourge spends enough bodies to bridge the silence.
- Failure is visible and physical. A tendril crosses the ash, a cable lights, the Beacon legs
  lift out of the ground, and the local horde snaps back into coordination.

## Sprite / Item Brief

- A brutal collapsible tripod: squat gunmetal legs, cracked bone-conductor feet, scorched
  ceramic cautery cells around a black ash core, and a small forked marker vane that trembles
  toward the nearest signal.
- Warden variant: heavy sled-mounted beacon with anchor spikes, hazard stencils, and a field
  battery built for lane work.
- Pyre variant: compact coffin-beacon with scorch tape, pull-ring igniter, ash seals, and a
  backplate made to be dumped while running.
- Toxic-green appears only as incoming signal bleed dying at the ash ring. The human device
  stays rust, blood, gunmetal, bone, and hellfire per [[Style-Bible]].

## Appears in

- [[Deadlane]] - Warden crews plant Beacons after a repeater cut to keep a lane segment dark
  long enough to rebuild walls, rotate guns, or move a crew through a gap before the Choir
  re-roots.
- [[Redline]] - couriers trigger road Beacons while crossing [[The-Hollow-Lanes|the Hollow
  Lanes]], forcing a pylon-seeded junction into dead air for the few seconds needed to stay at
  redline.
- [[Scourge-Survivors]] - Pyre Purgers drop coffin-beacons in [[Perdition]] corridors to hold
  an extraction path open after a [[Blackout-Nail]] cuts the local carrier.
- [[The-Hush]] - used rarely and brutally; the holdout already survives by giving the Choir
  almost nothing to hear, so burning a permanent dead-air scar inside its walls is treated as
  last-resort surgery.
