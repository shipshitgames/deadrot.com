# Tuning Fork

**At a glance:** Pact-shared field tech · a passive receiver that hears the **Choir** and ranges its repeaters · finds the node you have to sever ([[CANON]] §6) · appears in [[Deadlane]] / [[Scourge-Survivors]] / [[Starblight]].

You cannot sever what you cannot find. The Scourge's one weakness is **isolation** — kill its
[[Chorister|repeaters]] or push a node past the edge of its radius and the local horde drops
to feral ([[CANON]] §6) — but the repeaters do not stand in the open. A [[Chorister]] hides
behind the swarm it empowers; a [[Cantor]] runs buried in the pack; a [[Descant]] sits behind
an escort screen; a [[Bourdon]] is sunk in the rock. The horde *is* the wall around the thing
worth killing. The **Tuning Fork** is how humanity sees through it.

It is a passive receiver — old-world signals gear, salvaged and re-grown after [[Zero-Day]] —
that catches the carrier the Choir broadcasts on ([[CANON]] §5) and **rings toward its loudest
source**: the nearest repeater. A forked resonator of gunmetal and bone-conductor hums in the
carrier's hand, faint and far off, building to a skull-deep toll when it points true. Where the
ring falls to nothing, you've found the **edge of the radius** — the dead air where a node can
be pushed beyond the Choir and severed without ever landing a blow. It does not jam the signal
and it does not cut it. It only tells you where the wound is. The killing is still yours to do,
still through the swarm, still up close — which is the whole point ([[CANON]] §6). The Fork buys
you the target; it never buys you the win.

It is the rare thing both orders carry. The Fork predates **The Schism** — it was already in the
old armories when the [[The-Pyre|Pyre]] broke from the [[The-Wardens|Wardens]] — so under
**The Pact** both run the same rig. Wardens mount heavy fixed Forks on the wall to range a lane
before a wave commits ([[Deadlane]]); Pyre Purgers carry a gutted hand unit *into* the breach,
hunting the toll down the dark toward the node ([[Scourge-Survivors]] / [[Perdition]]).

## The cost

To range the Choir you have to *listen* to it, and the Choir is a hive mind singing with the
voices of every world it has eaten ([[CANON]] §2). Operators on long Fork duty bleed from the
ears, lose sleep, and start finishing the sentences of the dead. The Wardens rotate listeners
hard and burn out the ones who linger; the Pyre treat a Fork operator who hears too clearly as
already lost. This is the crack the [[The-Listeners]] widen into a creed: the Fork only *ranges*
the song to kill it faster — the heresy asks what the song is **saying**, and tries to answer
back ([[CANON]] §8). Same signal, same gear, one short step from a targeting tool to a
conversation nobody sanctioned.

## Gameplay Read

- A **detection / objective-marker layer**, not a weapon. The Fork surfaces the repeater the
  encounter is built around reaching — diegetic, no floating UI: a directional rising tone and a
  rumble that peaks pointed at the node.
- Reads "hot/cold" and **directional**: louder toward the repeater, dead at the radius edge. The
  skill is triangulating and pushing in, then fighting through the coordinated swarm to the node.
- Falls **deaf in dense swarm-noise** — packed Scourge drown the carrier, so the Fork is least
  use exactly where the horde is thickest. You earn the range; you don't get it free.
- Cross-front consistency: it's *why* a [[Deadlane]] crew can prioritise the [[Cantor]] in a
  wave, why a Purger can find the [[Bourdon]] in the deep, and why a [[Starblight]] pilot can
  pick the [[Descant]] out of the escort screen. One tool, one win condition, every game.

## Sprite / Item Brief

- A forked resonator rod — gunmetal tines wrapped in salvaged old-world wiring, a host-bone
  conductor lashed into the haft where the parasite carrier couples best. Field-grim, riveted,
  not clean tech.
- Toxic-green is the *signal it catches*, never the device: a single sickly node glows in the
  fork's throat brighter as it nears a repeater — Scourge-only colour ([[Style-Bible]]) bleeding
  into a human tool, the one green light a human is allowed to hold.
- Heavy Warden wall-mount variant (braced, multi-tined, crewed) and a gutted Pyre hand variant
  (stripped, taped, one-hand, carried into the dark).

## Prompt Seed

DOOM-grim handheld signals device, a forked resonator rod of riveted gunmetal tines wrapped in
salvaged old-world wiring with a host-bone conductor lashed into the haft, a single sickly
toxic-green node glowing in the fork's throat, blood and rust and scorched metal, heavy shadows,
gritty military field-tech, no neon, single object on near-black background.

## Appears in

- [[Deadlane]] — fixed Warden wall-Forks range a [[The-Hollow-Lanes|lane]] before a wave commits,
  marking the repeater ([[Chorister]] / [[Cantor]]) so the crew knows which node to break to send
  the wave feral.
- [[Scourge-Survivors]] — a Pyre Purger's hand unit tolling down the dark toward the
  [[Chorister]] / [[Bourdon]] deep in [[Perdition]]; the deeper you go, the louder it sings and
  the more it costs to keep listening.
- [[Starblight]] — pilot-rig tuned up the gravity well to pick the [[Descant]] out of its escort
  screen, the high node to sever to blind the falling wave.
